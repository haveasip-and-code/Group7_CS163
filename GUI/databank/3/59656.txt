whack
To strike; to beat; to give a heavy or resounding blow to; to thrash; to make with whacks. [Colloq.] Rodsmen were whackingtheir way through willow brakes. G. W. Cable.\n\nTo strike anything with a smart blow. To whack away, to continue striking heavy blows; as, to whack away at a log. [Colloq.]\n\nA smart resounding blow. [Colloq.]
