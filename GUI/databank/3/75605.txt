disarray
1. To throw into disorder; to break the array of. Who with fiery steeds Oft disarrayed the foes in battle ranged. Fenton. 2. To take off the dress of; to unrobe. So, as she bade, the witch they disarrayed. Spenser.\n\n1. Want of array or regular order; disorder; confusion. Disrank the troops, set all in disarray. Daniel. 2. Confused attire; undress. Spenser.
