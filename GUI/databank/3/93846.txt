tympan
1. A drum. [Obs.] 2. (Arch.)  A panel; a tympanum. 3. (Print.)  A frame covered with parchment or cloth, on which the blank sheets are put, in order to be laid on the form to be impressed. Tympan sheet (Print.), a sheet of paper of the same size as that to be printed, pasted on the tympan, and serving as a guide in laying the sheets evenly for printing. W. Savage.
