drown
To be suffocated in water or other fluid; to perish in water. Methought, what pain it was to drown. Shak.\n\n1. To overwhelm in water; to submerge; to inundate. \"They drown the land.\" Dryden. 2. To deprive of life by immersion in water or other liquid. 3. To overpower; to overcome; to extinguish; -- said especially of sound. Most men being in sensual pleasures drowned. Sir J. Davies. My private voice is drowned amid the senate. Addison. To drown up, to swallow up. [Obs.] Holland.
