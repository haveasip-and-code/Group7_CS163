crimson
A deep red color tinged with blue; also, red color in general. Theugh jour be as scarlet, they shall be as white as snow; though they be red like crimson, they shall be as wool. Is. i. 18. A maid jet rosed over with the virgin crimson of modesty. Shak.\n\nOf a deep red color tinged with blue; deep red. \"A crimson tide.\" Mrs. Hemans. The blushing poppy with a crimson hue. Prior.\n\nTo dye with crimson or deep red; to redden. Signed in thy spoil and crimsoned in thy lethe. Shak.\n\nTo become crimson; to blush. Ancient towers . . . beginning to crimson with the radiant luster of a cloudless July morning. De Quincey.
