fuss
1. A tumult; a bustle; unnecessary or annoying ado about trifles. Byron. Zealously, assiduously, and with a minimum of fuss or noise Carlyle. 2. One who is unduly anxious about trifles. [R.] I am a fuss and I don't deny it. W. D. Howell.\n\nTo be overbusy or unduly anxious about trifles; to make a bustle or ado. Sir W. Scott.
