dudgeon
1. The root of the box tree, of which hafts for daggers were made. Gerarde (1597). 2. The haft of a dagger. Shak. 3. A dudgeon-hafted dagger; a dagger. Hudibras.\n\nResentment; ill will; anger; displeasure. I drink it to thee in dudgeon and hostility. Sir T. Scott.\n\nHomely; rude; coarse. [Obs.] By my troth, though I am plain and dudgeon, I would not be an ass. Beau. & Fl.
