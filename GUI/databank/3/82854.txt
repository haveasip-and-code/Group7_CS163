poundage
1. A sum deducted from a pound, or a certain sum paid for each pound; a commission. 2. A subsidy of twelve pence in the pound, formerly granted to the crown on all goods exported or imported, and if by aliens, more. [Eng.] Blackstone. 3. (Law)  The sum allowed to a sheriff or other officer upon the amount realized by an execution; -- estimated in England, and formerly in the United States, at so much of the pound. Burrill. Bouvier.\n\nTo collect, as poundage; to assess, or rate, by poundage. [R.]\n\n1. Confinement of cattle, or other animals, in a public pound. 2. A charge paid for the release of impounded cattle.
