canopy
1. A covering fixed over a bed, dais, or the like, or carried on poles over an exalted personage or a sacred object, etc. chiefly as a mark of honor. \"Golden canoniec and beds of state.\" Dryden. 2. (Arch.) (a) An ornamental projection, over a door, window, niche, etc. (b) Also, a roofike covering, supported on pilars over an altar, a statue, a fountain, etc.\n\nTo cover with, or as with, a canopy. \"A bank with ivy canopied.\" Milton.
