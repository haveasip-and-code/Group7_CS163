meed
1. That which is bestowed or rendered in consideration of merit; reward; recompense. A rosy garland was the victor's meed. Spenser. 2. Merit or desert; worth. My meed hath got me fame. Shak. 3. A gift; also, a bride. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\n1. To reward; to repay. [Obs.] Waytt. 2. To deserve; to merit. [Obs.] Heywood.
