tiff
1. Liquor; especially, a small draught of liquor. \"Sipping his tiff of brandy punch.\" Sir W. Scott. 2. A fit of anger or peevishness; a slight altercation or contention. See Tift. Thackeray.\n\nTo be in a pet. She tiffed with Tim, she ran from Ralph. Landor.\n\nTo deck out; to dress. [Obs.] A. Tucker.
