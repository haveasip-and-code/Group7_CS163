soluble
1. Susceptible of being dissolved in a fluid; capable of solution; as, some substances are soluble in alcohol which are not soluble in water. Sugar is . . . soluble in water and fusible in fire. Arbuthnot. 2. Susceptible of being solved; as, a soluble algebraic problem; susceptible of being disentangled, unraveled, or explained; as, the mystery is perhaps soluble. \"More soluble is this knot.\" Tennyson. 3. Relaxed; open or readily opened. [R.] \"The bowels must be kept soluble.\" Dunglison. Soluble glass. (Chem.) See under Glass.
