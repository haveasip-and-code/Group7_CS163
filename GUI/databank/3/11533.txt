cavalier
1. A military man serving on horseback; a knight. 2. A gay, sprightly, military man; hence, a gallant. 3. One of the court party in the time of king Charles L. as contrasted with a Roundhead or an adherent of Parliament. Clarendon. 4. (Fort.)  A work of more that ordinary heigh, rising from the level ground of a bastion, etc., and overlooking surrounding parts.\n\nGay; easy; offhand; frank. The plodding, persevering scupulous accuracy of the one, and the easy, cavalier, verbal fluency of the other, from a complete contrast. Hazlitt. 2. High-spirited. [Obs.] \"The people are naturally not valiant, and not much cavalier.\" Suckling. 3. Supercilious; haughty; disdainful; curt; brusque. 4. Of or pertaining to the party of King Charles I. \"An old Cavalier family.\" Beaconsfleld.
