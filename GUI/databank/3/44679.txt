bunch
1. A protuberance; a hunch; a knob or lump; a hump. They will carry . . . their treasures upon the bunches of camels. Isa. xxx. 6. 2. A collection, cluster, or tuft, properly of things of the same kind, growing or fastened together; as, a bunch of grapes; a bunch of keys. 3. (Mining)  A small isolated mass of ore, as distinguished from a continuous vein. Page.\n\nTo swell out into a bunch or protuberance; to be protuberant or round. Bunching out into a large round knob at one end. Woodward.\n\nTo form into a bunch or bunches.
