naiad
1. (Myth.)  A water nymph; one of the lower female divinities, fabled to preside over some body of fresh water, as a lake, river, brook, or fountain. 2. (Zoöl.)  Any species of a tribe (Naiades) of freshwater bivalves, including Unio, Anodonta, and numerous allied genera; a river mussel. 3. (Zoöl)  One of a group of butterflies. See Nymph. 4. (Bot.)  Any plant of the order Naiadaceæ, such as eelgrass, pondweed, etc.
