thrid
Third. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\n1. To pass through in the manner of a thread or a needle; to make or find a course through; to thread. Some thrid the mazy ringlets of her hair. Pope. And now he thrids the bramble bush. J. R. Drake. I began To thrid the musky-circled mazes. Tennyson. 2. To make or effect (a way or course) through something; as, to thrid one's way through a wood.\n\nThread; continuous line. [Archaic] I resume the thrid of my discourse. Dryden.
