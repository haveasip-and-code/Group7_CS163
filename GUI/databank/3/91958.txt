scape
1. (Bot.)  A peduncle rising from the ground or from a subterranean stem, as in the stemless violets, the bloodroot, and the like. 2. (Zoöl.)  The long basal joint of the antennæ of an insect. 3. (Arch.) (a) The shaft of a column. (b) The apophyge of a shaft.\n\nTo escape. [Obs. or Poetic.] Milton. Out of this prison help that we may scape. Chaucer.\n\n1. An escape. [Obs.] I spake of most disastrous chances, . . . Of hairbreadth scapes in the imminent, deadly breach. Shak. 2. Means of escape; evasion. [Obs.] Donne. 3. A freak; a slip; a fault; an escapade. [Obs.] Not pardoning so much as the scapes of error and ignorance. Milton. 4. Loose act of vice or lewdness. [Obs.] Shak.
