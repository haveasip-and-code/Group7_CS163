beck
See Beak. [Obs.] Spenser.\n\nA small brook. The brooks, the becks, the rills. Drayton.\n\nA vat. See Back.\n\nTo nod, or make a sign with the head or hand. [Archaic] Drayton.\n\nTo notify or call by a nod, or a motion of the head or hand; to intimate a command to. [Archaic] When gold and silver becks me to come on. Shak.\n\nA significant nod, or motion of the head or hand, esp. as a call or command. They have troops of soldiers at their beck. Shak.
