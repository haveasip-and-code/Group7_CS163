cheek
1. The side of the face below the eye. 2. The cheek bone. [Obs.] Caucer. 3. pl. (Mech.)  Those pieces of a machine, or of any timber, or stone work, which form corresponding sides, or which are similar and in pair; as, the cheeks (jaws) of a vise; the cheeks of a gun carriage, etc. 4. pl.  The branches of a bridle bit. Knight. 5. (Founding)  A section of a flask, so made that it can be moved laterally, to permit the removal of the pattern from the mold; the middle part of a flask. 6. Cool confidence; assurance; impudence. [Slang] Cheek of beef.
