commotion
1. Disturbed or violent motion; agitation. [What] commotion in the winds! Shak. 2. A popular tumult; public disturbance; riot. When ye shall hear of wars and commotions. Luke xxi. 9. 3. Agitation, perturbation, or disorder, of mind; heat; excitement. \"He could not debate anything without some commotion.\" Clarendon. Syn. -- Excitement; agitation; perturbation; disturbance; tumult; disorder; violence.
