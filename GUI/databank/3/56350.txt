tinkle
The common guillemot. [Prov. Eng.]\n\n1. To make, or give forth, small, quick, sharp sounds, as a piece of metal does when struck; to clink. As sounding brass, or a tinkling cymbal. 1 Cor. xiii. 1. The sprightly horse Moves to the music of his tinkling bells. Dodsley. 2. To hear, or resound with, a small, sharp sound. And his ears tinkled, and the color fled. Dryden.\n\nTo cause to clonk, or make small, sharp, quick sounds.\n\nA small, sharp, quick sound, as that made by striking metal. Cowper.
