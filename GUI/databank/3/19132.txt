feasible
1. Capable of being done, executed, or effected; practicable. Always existing before their eyes as a thing feasible in practice. Burke. It was not feasible to gratify so many ambitions. Beaconsfield. 2. Fit to be used or tailed, as land. [R.] R. Trumbull. Fea\"si*ble*ness, n. --Fea\"si*bly, adv.
