repine
1. To fail; to wane. [Obs.] \"Reppening courage yields no foot to foe.\" Spenser. 2. To continue pining; to feel inward discontent which preys on the spirits; to indulge in envy or complaint; to murmur. But Lachesis thereat gan to repine. Spenser. What if the head, the eye, or ear repined To serve mere engines to the ruling mind Pope.\n\nVexation; mortification. [Obs.] Shak.
