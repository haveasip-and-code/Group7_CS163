surd
1. Net having the sense of hearing; deaf. [Obs.] \"A surd . . . generation.\" Sir T. Browne. 2. Unheard. [Obs.] Kenrick. 3. (Math.)  Involving surds; not capable of being expressed in rational numbers; radical; irrational; as, a surd expression or quantity; a surd number. 4. (Phonetics)  Uttered, as an element of speech, without tone, or proper vocal sound; voiceless; unintonated; nonvocal; atonic; whispered; aspirated; sharp; hard, as f, p, s, etc.; -- opposed to sonant. See Guide to Pronunciation, §§169, 179, 180.\n\n1. A quantity which can not be expressed by rational numbers; thus, *2 is a surd. 2. (Phon.)  A surd element of speech. See Surd, a., 4.
