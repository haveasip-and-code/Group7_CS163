ken
A house; esp., one which is a resort for thieves. [Slang, Eng.]\n\n1. To know; to understand; to take cognizance of. [Archaic or Scot.] 2. To recognize; to descry; to discern. [Archaic or Scot.] \"We ken them from afar.\" Addison 'T is he. I ken the manner of his gait. Shak.\n\nTo look around. [Obs.] Burton.\n\nCognizance; view; especially, reach of sight or knowledge. \"Beyond his ken.\" Longfellow. Above the reach and ken of a mortal apprehension. South. It was relief to quit the ken And the inquiring looks of men. Trench.
