bugle
A sort of wild ox; a buffalo. E. Phillips.\n\n1. A horn used by hunters. 2. (Mus.)  A copper instrument of the horn quality of tone, shorter and more conical that the trumpet, sometimes keyed; formerly much used in military bands, very rarely in the orchestra; now superseded by the cornet; -- called also the Kent bugle.\n\nAn elingated glass bead, of various colors, though commonly black.\n\nJet black. \"Bugle eyeballs.\" Shak.\n\nA plant of the genus Ajuga of the Mint family, a native of the Old World.
