tine
Trouble; distress; teen. [Obs.] \"Cruel winter's tine.\" Spenser.\n\nTo kindle; to set on fire. [Obs.] See Tind. \"To tine the cloven wood.\" Dryden. Coals of contention and hot vegneance tind. Spenser.\n\nTo kindle; to rage; to smart. [Obs.] Ne was there slave, ne was there medicine That mote recure their wounds; so inly they did tine. Spenser.\n\nTo shut in, or inclose. [Prov. Eng.] Halliwell.\n\nA tooth, or spike, as of a fork; a prong, as of an antler.
