steeve
To project upward, or make an angle with the horizon or with the line of a vessel's keel; -- said of the bowsprit, etc.\n\n1. (Shipbuilding)  To elevate or fix at an angle with the horizon; -- said of the bowsprit, etc. 2. To stow, as bales in a vessel's hold, by means of a steeve. See Steeve, n. (b).\n\n(a) The angle which a bowsprit makes with the horizon, or with the line of the vessel's keel; -- called also steeving. (b) A spar, with a block at one end, used in stowing cotton bales, and similar kinds of cargo which need to be packed tightly.
