sulky
Moodly silent; sullen; sour; obstinate; morose; splenetic. Syn. -- See Sullen.\n\nA light two-wheeled carriage for a single person. Note: Sulky is used adjectively in the names of several agricultural machines drawn by horses to denote that the machine is provided with wheels and a seat for the driver; as, sulky plow; sulky harrow; sulky rake, etc.
