postern
1. Originally, a back door or gate; a private entrance; hence, any small door or gate. He by a privy postern took his flight. Spenser. Out at the postern, by the abbey wall. Shak. 2. (Fort.)  A subterraneous passage communicating between the parade and the main ditch, or between the ditches and the interior of the outworks. Mahan.\n\nBack; being behind; private. \"The postern door.\" Dryden.
