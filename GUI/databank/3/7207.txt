expedite
1. Free of impediment; unimpeded. To make the way plain and expedite. Hooker. 2. Expeditious; quick; speedily; prompt. Nimble and expedite . . . in its operation. Tollotson. Speech is a very short and expedite way of conveying their thoughts. Locke.\n\n1. To relieve of impediments; to facilitate; to accelerate the process or progress of; to hasten; to quicken; as, to expedite the growth of plants. To expedite your glorious march. Milton. 2. To despatch; to send forth; to issue officially. Such charters be expedited of course. Bacon.
