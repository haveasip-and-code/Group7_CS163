palpable
1. Capable of being touched and felt; perceptible by the touch; as, a palpable form. Shak. Darkness must overshadow all his bounds, Palpable darkness. Milton. 2. Easily perceptible; plain; distinct; obvious; readily perceived and detected; gross; as, palpable imposture; palpable absurdity; palpable errors. \"Three persons palpable.\" P. Plowman. [Lies] gross as a mountain, open, palpable. Shak. -- Pal\"pa*ble*ness, n. -- Pal\"pa*bly, adv.
