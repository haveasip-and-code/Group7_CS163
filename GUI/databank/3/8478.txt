pudder
To make a tumult or bustle; to splash; to make a pother or fuss; to potter; to meddle. Puddering in the designs or doings of others. Barrow. Others pudder into their food with their broad nebs. Holland.\n\nTo perplex; to embarrass; to confuse; to bother; as, to pudder a man. Locke.\n\nA pother; a tumult; a confused noise; turmoil; bustle. \"All in a pudder.\" Milton.
