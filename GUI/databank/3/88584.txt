impair
To make worse; to diminish in quantity, value, excellence, or strength; to deteriorate; as, to impair health, character, the mind, value. Time sensibly all things impairs. Roscommon. In years he seemed, but not impaired by years. Pope. Syn. -- To diminish; decrease; injure; weaken; enfeeble; debilitate; reduce; debase; deteriorate.\n\nTo grow worse; to deteriorate. Milton.\n\nNot fit or appropriate. [Obs.]\n\nDiminution; injury. [Obs.]
