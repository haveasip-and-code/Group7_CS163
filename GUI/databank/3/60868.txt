conge
To take leave with the customary civilities; to bow or courtesy. I have congeed with the duke, done my adieu with his  nearest. Shak.\n\n1. The act of taking leave; parting ceremony; farewell; also, dismissal. Should she pay off old Briggs and give her her congé Thackeray. 2.  The customary act of civility on any occasion; a bow or a courtesy. The captain salutes you with congé profound. Swift. 3.  (Arch.) An apophyge. Gwilt. Congé d'élire [F., leave to choose] (Eccl.), the sovereign's license or permission to a dean and chapter to choose as bishop the person nominated in the missive.
