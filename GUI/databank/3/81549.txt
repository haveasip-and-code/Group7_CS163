doff
1. To put off, as dress; to divest one's self of; hence, figuratively, to put or thrust away; to rid one's self of. And made us doff our easy robes of peace. Shak. At night, or in the rain, He dons a surcoat which he doffs at morn. Emerson. 2. To strip; to divest; to undress. Heaven's King, who doffs himself our flesh to wear. Crashaw.\n\nTo put off dress; to take off the hat.
