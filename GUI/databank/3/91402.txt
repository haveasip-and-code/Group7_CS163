exceeding
More than usual; extraordinary; more than sufficient; measureless. \"The exceeding riches of his grace.\" Eph. ii. 7. -- Ex*ceed\"ing*ness, n. [Obs.] Sir P. Sidney.\n\nIn a very great degree; extremely; exceedingly. [Archaic. It is not joined to verbs.] \"The voice exceeding loud.\" Keble. His raiment became shining, exceeding white as snow. Mark ix. 3. The Genoese were exceeding powerful by sea. Sir W. Raleigh.
