actuate
1. To put into action or motion; to move or incite to action; to influence actively; to move as motives do; -- more commonly used of persons. Wings, which others were contriving to actuate by the perpetual motion. Johnson. Men of the greatest abilities are most fired with ambition; and, on the contrary, mean and narrow minds are the least actuated by it. Addison. 2. To carry out in practice; to perform. [Obs.] \"To actuate what you command.\" Jer. Taylor. Syn. -- To move; impel; incite; rouse; instigate; animate.\n\nPut in action; actuated. [Obs.] South.
