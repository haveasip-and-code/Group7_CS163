climb
1. To ascend or mount laboriously, esp. by use of the hands and feet. 2. To ascend as if with effort; to rise to a higher point. Black vapors climb aloft, and cloud the day. Dryden. 3. (Bot.)  To ascend or creep upward by twining about a support, or by attaching itself by tendrills, rootlets, etc., to a support or upright surface.\n\nTo ascend, as by means of the hands and feet, or laboriously or slowly; to mount.\n\nThe act of one who climbs; ascent by climbing. Warburton.
