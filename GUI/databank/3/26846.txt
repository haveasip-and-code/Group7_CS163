feeble
1. Deficient in physical strenght; weak; infirm; debilitated. Carried all the feeble of them upon asses. 2 Chron. xxviii. 15. 2. Wanting force, vigor, or efficiency in action or expression; not full, loud, bright, strong, rapid, etc.; faint; as, a feeble color; feeble motion. \"A lady's feeble voice.\" Shak.\n\nTo make feble; to enfeeble. [Obs.] Shall that victorious hand be feebled here Shak.
