sist
1. (Scots Law)  To stay, as judicial proceedings; to delay or suspend; to stop. 2. To cause to take a place, as at the bar of a court; hence, to cite; to summon; to bring into court. [Scot.] Some, however, have preposterously sisted nature as the first or generative principle. Sir W. Hamilton.\n\nA stay or suspension of proceedings; an order for a stay of proceedings. Burril.
