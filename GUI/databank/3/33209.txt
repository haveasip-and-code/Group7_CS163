broadside
1. (Naut.)  The side of a ship above the water line, from the bow to the quarter. 2. A discharge of or from all the guns on one side of a ship, at the same time. 3. A volley of abuse or denunciation. [Colloq.] 4. (Print.)  A sheet of paper containing one large page, or printed on one side only; -- called also broadsheet.
