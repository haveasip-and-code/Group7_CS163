cognation
1. Relationship by blood; descent from the same original; kindred. As by our cognation to the body of the first Adam. Jer. Taylor. 2. Participation of the same nature. Sir T. Browne. A like temper and cognation. Sir K. Digby. 3. (Law)  That tie of consanguinity which exists between persons descended from the same mother; -- used in distinction from agnation.
