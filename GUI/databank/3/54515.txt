statuary
1. One who practices the art of making statues. On other occasions the statuaries took their subjects from the poets. Addison. 2. Etym: [L. statuaria (sc. ars): cf. F. statuaire.]  The art of carving statues or images as representatives of real persons or things; a branch of sculpture. Sir W. Temple. 3. A collection of statues; statues, collectively.
