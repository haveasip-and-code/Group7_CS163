her
The form of the objective and the possessive case of the personal pronoun she; as, I saw her with her purse out. Note: The possessive her takes the form hers when the noun with which in agrees is not given, but implied. \"And what his fortune wanted, hers could mend.\" Dryden.\n\nOf them; their. [Obs.] Piers Plowman. On here bare knees adown they fall. Chaucer.
