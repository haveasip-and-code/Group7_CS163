spud
1. A sharp, narrow spade, usually with a long handle, used by farmers for digging up large-rooted weeds; a similarly shaped implement used for various purposes. My spud these nettles from the stone can part. Swyft. 2. A dagger. [Obs.] olland. 3. Anything short and thick; specifically, a piece of dough boiled in fat. [Local, U.S.]
