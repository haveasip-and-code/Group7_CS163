cleek
1. A large hook or crook, as for a pot over a fire; specif., an iron- headed golf club with a straight, narrow face and a long shaft. 2.  Act of cleeking; a clutch. [Scot.]\n\n1.  To seize; clutch; snatch; catch; pluck. 2.  To catch or draw out with a cleek, as a fish; to hook. 3. To hook or link (together); hence, to marry.  Scott.
