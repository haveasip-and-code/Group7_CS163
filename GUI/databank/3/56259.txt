levee
1. The act of rising. \" The sun's levee.\" Gray. 2. A morning assembly or reception of visitors, -- in distinction from a soirée, or evening assembly; a matinée; hence, also, any general or somewhat miscellaneous gathering of guests, whether in the daytime or evening; as, the president's levee. Note: In England a ceremonious day reception, when attended by both ladies and gentlemen, is called a drawing-room.\n\nTo attend the levee or levees of. He levees all the great. Young.\n\nAn embankment to prevent inundation; as, the levees along the Mississippi; sometimes, the steep bank of a river. [U. S. ]\n\nTo keep within a channel by means of levees; as, to levee a river. [U. S.]
