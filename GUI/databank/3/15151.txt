withe
1. A flexible, slender twig or branch used as a band; a willow or osier twig; a withy. 2. A band consisting of a twig twisted. 3. (Naut.)  An iron attachment on one end of a mast or boom, with a ring, through which another mast or boom is rigged out and secured; a wythe. R. H. Dana, Jr. 4. (Arch.)  A partition between flues in a chimney.\n\nTo bind or fasten with withes. You shall see him withed, and haltered, and staked, and baited to death. Bp. Hall.
