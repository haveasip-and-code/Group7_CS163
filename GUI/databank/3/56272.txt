mete
Meat. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\nTo meet. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\nTo dream; also impersonally; as, me mette, I dreamed. [Obs.] \"I mette of him all night.\" Chaucer.\n\nTo find the quantity, dimensions, or capacity of, by any rule or standard; to measure.\n\nTo measure. [Obs.] Mark iv. 24.\n\nMeasure; limit; boundary; -- used chiefly in the plural, and in the phrase metes and bounds.
