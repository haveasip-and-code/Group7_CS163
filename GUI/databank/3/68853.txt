trig
To fill; to stuff; to cram. [Obs.] Dr. H. More.\n\nFull; also, trim; neat. [Prov. Eng. & Scot.] To sit on a horse square and trig. Brit. Quart. Rev.\n\nTo stop, as a wheel, by placing something under it; to scotch; to skid.\n\nA stone, block of wood, or anything else, placed under a wheel or barrel to prevent motion; a scotch; a skid. [Eng.] Wright.
