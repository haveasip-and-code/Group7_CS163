curdle
1. To change into curd; to coagulate; as, rennet causes milk to curdle. Thomson. 2. To thicken; to congeal. Then Mary could feel her heart's blood curdle cold. Southey.\n\n1. To change into curd; to cause to coagulate. \"To curdle whites of eggs\" Boyle. 2. To congeal or thicken. My chill blood is curdled in my veins. Dryden.
