nigh
1. Not distant or remote in place or time; near. The loud tumult shows the battle nigh. Prior. 2. Not remote in degree, kindred, circumstances, etc.; closely allied; intimate. \"Nigh kinsmen.\" Knolles. Ye ... are made nigh by the blood of Christ. Eph. ii. 13. Syn. -- Near; close; adjacent; contiguous; present; neighboring.\n\n1. In a situation near in place or time, or in the course of events; near. He was sick, nigh unto death. Phil. ii. 27. He drew not nigh unheard; the angel bright, Ere he drew nigh, his radiant visage turned. Milton. 2. Almost; nearly; as, he was nigh dead.\n\nTo draw nigh (to); to approach; to come near. [Obs.] Wyclif (Matt. iii. 2).\n\nNear to; not remote or distant from. \"was not this nigh shore\" Shak.
