casket
1. A small chest or box, esp. of rich material or ornamental character, as for jewels, etc. The little casket bring me hither. Shak. 2. A kind of burial case. [U. S.] 3. Anything containing or intended to contain something highly esteemed; as: (a) The body. (Shak). (b) The tomb. (Milton). (c) A book of selections. [poetic] They found him dead . . . an empty casket. Shak.\n\nA gasket. See Gasket.\n\nTo put into, or preserve in, a casket. [Poetic] \"I have casketed my treasure.\" Shak.
