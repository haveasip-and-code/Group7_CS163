dabble
To wet by little dips or strokes; to spatter; to sprinkle; to moisten; to wet. \"Bright hair dabbled in blood.\" Shak.\n\n1. To play in water, as with the hands; to paddle or splash in mud or water. Wher the duck dabbles Wordsworth. 2. To work in slight or superficial manner; to do in a small way; to tamper; to meddle. \"Dabbling here and there with the text.\" Atterbury. During the ferst year at Dumfries, Burns for the ferst time began to dabble in politics. J. C. Shairp.
