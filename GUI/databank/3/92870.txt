amble
1. To go at the easy gait called an amble; -- applied to the horse or to its rider. 2. To move somewhat like an ambling horse; to go easily or without hard shocks. The skipping king, he ambled up and down. Shak. Sir, your wit ambles well; it goes easily. Shak.\n\n1. A peculiar gait of a horse, in which both legs on the same side are moved at the same time, alternating with the legs on the other side. \"A fine easy amble.\" B. Jonson. 2. A movement like the amble of a horse.
