clinch
1. To hold firmly; to hold fast by grasping or embracing tightly. \"Clinch the pointed spear.\" Dryden. 2. To set closely together; to close tightly; as, to clinch the teeth or the first. Swift. 3. The bend or turn over the point of (something that has been driven trough an object), so that it will hold fast; as, to clinch a nail. 4. To make conclusive; to confirm; to establish; as, to clinch an argument. South.\n\nTo hold fast; to grasp something firmly; to seize or grasp one another.\n\n1. The act or process of holding fast; that which serves to hold fast; a grip; a grasp; a clamp; a holdfast; as, to get a good clinch of an antagonist, or of a weapon; to secure anything by a clinch. 2. A pun. Pope. 3. (Naut.)  A hitch or bend by which a rope is made fast to the ring of an anchor, or the breeching of a ship's gun to the ringbolts.
