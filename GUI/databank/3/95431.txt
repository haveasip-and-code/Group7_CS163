univocal
1. Having one meaning only; -- contrasted with equivocal. 2. Having unison of sound, as the octave in music. See Unison, n., 2. 3. Having always the same drift or tenor; uniform; certain; regular. [R.] Sir T. Browne. 4. Unequivocal; indubitable. [Obs.] Jer. Taylor.\n\n1. (Aristotelian Logic)  A generic term, or a term applicable in the same sense to all the species it embraces. 2. A word having but one meaning.
