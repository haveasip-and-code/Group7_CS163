carbonado
Flesh, fowl, etc., cut across, seasoned, and broiled on coals; a chop. [Obs.]\n\n1. To cut (meat) across for frying or broiling; to cut or slice and broil. [Obs.] A short-legged hen daintily carbonadoed. Bean. & Fl. 2. To cut or hack, as in fighting. [Obs.] I'll so carbonado your shanks. Shak.\n\nA black variety of diamond, found in Brazil, and used for diamond drills. It occurs in irregular or rounded fragments, rarely distinctly crystallized, with a texture varying from compact to porous.
