garish
1. Showy; dazzling; ostentatious; attracting or exciting attention. \"The garish sun.\" \"A garish flag.\" Shak. \"In . . . garish colors.\" Asham. \"The garish day.\" J. H. Newman. Garish like the laughters of drunkenness. Jer. Taylor. 2. Gay to extravagance; flighty. It makes the mind loose and garish. South. -- Gar\"ish*ly, adv. -- Garish*ness, n. Jer. Taylor.
