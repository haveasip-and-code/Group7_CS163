buckram
1. A coarse cloth of linen or hemp, stiffened with size or glue, used in garments to keep them in the form intended, and for wrappers to cover merchandise. Note: Buckram was formerly a very different material from that now known by the name. It was used for wearing apparel, etc. Beck (Draper's Dict. ). 2. (Bot.)  A plant. See Ramson. Dr. Prior.\n\n1. Made of buckram; as, a buckram suit. 2. Stiff; precise. \"Buckram dames.\" Brooke.\n\nTo strengthen with buckram; to make stiff. Cowper. BUCK'S-HORN Buck's\"-horn`, n. (Bot.)  A plant with leaves branched somewhat like a buck's horn (Plantago Coronopus); also, Lobelia coronopifolia.
