ravine
Food obtained by violence; plunder; prey; raven. \"Fowls of ravyne.\" Chaucer. Though Nature, red in tooth and claw With ravine, shrieked against his creed. Tennyson.\n\nSee Raven, v. t. & i.\n\n1. A torrent of water. [Obs.] Cotgrave. 2. A deep and narrow hollow, usually worn by a stream or torrent of water; a gorge; a mountain cleft.
