overblow
1. To blow over, or be subdued. [R.] Spenser. 2. (Mus.)  To force so much wind into a pipe that it produces an overtone, or a note higher than the natural note; thus, the upper octaves of a flute are produced by overblowing.\n\nTo blow away; to dissipate by wind, or as by wind. When this cloud of sorrow's overblown. Waller.
