dislodge
1. To drive from a lodge or place of rest; to remove from a place of quiet or repose; as, shells resting in the sea at a considerate depth are not dislodged by storms. 2. To drive out from a place of hiding or defense; as, to dislodge a deer, or an enemy. The Volscians are dislodg'd. Shak.\n\nTo go from a place of rest. [R.] Where Light and Darkness in perpetual round Lodge and dislodge by turns. Milton.\n\nDwelling apart; separation. [R.]
