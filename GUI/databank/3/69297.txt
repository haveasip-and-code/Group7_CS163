hike
To hike one's self; specif., to go with exertion or effort; to tramp; to march laboriously. [Dial. or Colloq.] \"If you persist in heaving and hiking like this.\"  Kipling. It's hike, hike, hike (march) till you stick in the mud, and then you hike back again a little slower than you went. Scribner's Mag.\n\nTo move with a swing, toss, throw, jerk, or the like. [Dial. or Colloq.]\n\nThe act of hiking; a tramp; a march. [Dial. or Colloq.] With every hike there's a few laid out with their hands crossed. Scribner's Mag.
