sundry
1. Several; divers; more than one or two; various. \"Sundry wines.\" Chaucer. \"Sundry weighty reasons.\" Shak. With many a sound of sundry melody. Chaucer. Sundry foes the rural realm surround. Dryden. 2. Separate; diverse. [Obs.] Every church almost had the Bible of a sundry translation. Coleridge. All and sundry, all collectively, and each separately.
