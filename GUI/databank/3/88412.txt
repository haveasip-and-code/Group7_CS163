deport
1. To transport; to carry away; to exile; to send into banishment. He told us he had been deported to Spain. Walsh. 2. To carry or demean; to conduct; to behave; -- followed by the reflexive pronoun. Let an ambassador deport himself in the most graceful manner befor a prince. Pope.\n\nBehavior; carrige; demeanor; deportment. [Obs.] \"Goddesslike deport.\" Milton.
