quoit
1. (a) A flattened ring-shaped piece of iron, to be pitched at a fixed object in play; hence, any heavy flat missile used for the same purpose, as a stone, piece of iron, etc. (b) pl.  A game played with quoits. Shak. 2. The discus of the ancients. See Discus. 3. A cromlech. [Prov. Eng.] J. Morley.\n\nTo throw quoits; to play at quoits. To quoit, to run, and steeds and chariots drive. Dryden.\n\nTo throw; to pitch. [Obs. or R.] Shak.
