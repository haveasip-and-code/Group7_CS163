roam
To go from place to place without any certain purpose or direction; to rove; to wander. He roameth to the carpenter's house. Chaucer. Daphne roaming through a thorny wood. Shak. Syn. -- To wander; rove; range; stroll; ramble.\n\nTo range or wander over. And now wild beasts came forth the woods to roam. Milton.\n\nThe act of roaming; a wandering; a ramble; as, he began his roam o'er hill amd dale. Milton.
