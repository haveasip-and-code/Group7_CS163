typical
1. Of the nature of a type; representing something by a form, model, or resemblance; emblematic; prefigurative. The Levitical priesthood was only typical of the Christian. Atterbury. 2. (Nat. Hist.)  Combining or exhibiting the essential characteristics of a group; as, a typical genus. -- Typ\"ic*al*ly, adv. -- Typ\"ic*al*ness, n.
