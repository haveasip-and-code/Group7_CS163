pap
1. (Anat.)  A nipple; a mammilla; a teat. Dryden. The paps which thou hast sucked. Luke xi. 27. 2. A rounded, nipplelike hill or peak; anything resembling a nipple in shape; a mamelon. Macaulay.\n\n1. A soft food for infants, made of bread boiled or softtened in milk or water. 2. Nourishment or support from official patronage; as, treasury pap. [Colloq. & Contemptuous] 3. The pulp of fruit. Ainsworth.\n\nTo feed with pap. Beau. & Fl.
