lither
Bad; wicked; false; worthless; slothful. [Obs.] Chaucer. Not lither in business, fervent in spirit. Bp. Woolton. Note: Professor Skeat thinks \" the lither sky\" as found in Shakespeare's Henry VI. ((Part I. IY. YII., 21) means the stagnant or pestilential sky. -- Li\"ther*ly, adv. [Obs.]. -- Li\"ther*ness, n. [Obs.]
