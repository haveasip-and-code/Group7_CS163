purfle
1. To decorate with a wrought or flowered border; to embroider; to ornament with metallic threads; as, to purfle with blue and white. P. Plowman. A goodly lady clad in scarlet red, Purfled with gold and pearl of rich assay. Spenser. 2. (Her.)  To ornament with a bordure of emines, furs, and the like; also, with gold studs or mountings.\n\n1. A hem, border., or trimming, as of embroidered work. 2. (Her.)  A border of any heraldic fur.
