sward
1. Skin; covering. [Obs. or Prov. Eng.] Halliwell. 2. The grassy surface of land; that part of the soil which is filled with the roots of grass; turf. The sward was trim as any garden lawn. Tennyson. Sward pork, bacon in large fitches. [Prov. Eng.]\n\nTo produce sward upon; to cover, or be covered, with sward. Mortimer.
