fist
1. The hand with the fingers doubled into the palm; the closed hand, especially as clinched tightly for the purpose of striking a blow. Who grasp the earth and heaven with my fist. Herbert. 2. The talons of a bird of prey. [Obs.] More light than culver in the falcon's fist. Spenser. 3. (print.)  the index mark [], used to direct special attention to the passage which follows. Hand over fist (Naut.), rapidly; hand over hand.\n\n1. To strike with the fist. Dryden. 2. To gripe with the fist. [Obs.] Shak.
