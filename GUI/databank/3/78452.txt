scamble
1. To move awkwardly; to be shuffling, irregular, or unsteady; to sprawl; to shamble. \"Some scambling shifts.\" Dr. H. More. \"A fine old hall, but a scambling house.\" Evelyn. 2. To move about pushing and jostling; to be rude and turbulent; to scramble. \"The scambling and unquiet time did push it out of . . . question.\" Shak.\n\nTo mangle. [Obs.] Mortimer.
