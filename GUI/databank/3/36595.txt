empale
To make pale. [Obs.] No bloodless malady empales their face. G. Fletcher.\n\n1. To fence or fortify with stakes; to surround with a line of stakes for defense; to impale. All that dwell near enemies empale villages, to save themselves from surprise. Sir W. Raleigh. 2. To inclose; to surround. See Impale. 3. To put to death by thrusting a sharpened stake through the body. 4. (Her.)  Same as Impale.
