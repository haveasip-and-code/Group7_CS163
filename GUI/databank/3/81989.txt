underhand
1. Secret; clandestine; hence, mean; unfair; fraudulent. Addison. 2. (Baseball, Cricket, etc.)  Done, as pitching, with the hand lower than the shoulder, or, as bowling, with the hand lower than elbow.\n\n1. By secret means; in a clandestine manner; hence, by fraud; unfairly. Such mean revenge, committed underhand. Dryden. Baillie Macwheeble provided Janet, underhand, with meal for their maintenance. Sir W. Scott. 2. (Baseball, Cricket, etc.)  In an underhand manner; -- said of pitching or bowling.
