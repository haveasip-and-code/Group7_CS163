loan
A loanin. [Scot.]\n\n1. The act of lending; a lending; permission to use; as, the loan of a book, money, services. 2. That which one lends or borrows, esp. a sum of money lent at interest; as, he repaid the loan. Loan office. (a) An office at which loans are negotiated, or at which the accounts of loans are kept, and the interest paid to the lender. (b) A pawnbroker's shop.\n\nTo lend; -- sometimes with out. Kent. By way of location or loaning them out. J. Langley (1644).
