pageant
1. A theatrical exhibition; a spectacle. \"A pageant truly played.\" Shak. To see sad pageants of men's miseries. Spenser. 2. An elaborate exhibition devised for the entertainmeut of a distinguished personage, or of the public; a show, spectacle, or display. The gaze of fools, and pageant of a day ! Pope. We love the man, the paltry pageant you. Cowper.\n\nOf the nature of a pageant; spectacular. \"Pageant pomp.\" Dryden.\n\nTo exhibit in show; to represent; to mimic. [R.] \"He pageants us.\" Shak.
