smoulder
1. To burn and smoke without flame; to waste away by a slow and supressed combustion. The smoldering dust did round about him smoke. Spenser. 2. To exist in a state of suppressed or smothered activity; to burn inwardly; as, a smoldering feud.\n\nTo smother; to suffocate; to choke. [Obs.] Holinshed. Palsgrave.\n\nSmoke; smother. [Obs.] The smolder stops our nose with stench. Gascoigne.\n\nSee Smolder.
