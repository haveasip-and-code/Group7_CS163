tow
The coarse and broken part of flax or hemp, separated from the finer part by the hatchel or swingle.\n\nTo draw or pull through the water, as a vessel of any kind, by means of a rope.\n\n1. A rope by which anything is towed; a towline, or towrope. 2. The act of towing, or the state of being towed;-chiefly used in the phrase, to take in tow, that is to tow. 3. That which is towed, or drawn by a towline, as a barge, raft, collection of boats, ect.
