sneak
1. To creep or steal (away or about) privately; to come or go meanly, as a person afraid or ashamed to be seen; as, to sneak away from company. imp. & p. p. \"snuck\" is more common now, but not even mentioned here. In MW10, simply \"sneaked or snuck\" You skulked behind the fence, and sneaked away. Dryden. 2. To act in a stealthy and cowardly manner; to behave with meanness and servility; to crouch.\n\nTo hide, esp. in a mean or cowardly manner. [Obs.] \"[Slander] sneaks its head.\" Wake.\n\n1. A mean, sneaking fellow. A set of simpletons and superstitious sneaks. Glanvill. 2. (Cricket)  A ball bowled so as to roll along the ground; -- called also grub. [Cant] R. A. Proctor.
