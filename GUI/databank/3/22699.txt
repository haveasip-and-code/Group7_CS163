defiance
1. The act of defying, putting in opposition, or provoking to combat; a challenge; a provocation; a summons to combat. A war without a just defiance made. Dryden. Stood for her cause, and flung defiance down. Tennyson. 2. A state of opposition; willingness to flight; disposition to resist; contempt of opposition. He breathed defiance to my ears. Shak. 3. A casting aside; renunciation; rejection. [Obs.] \"Defiance to thy kindness.\" Ford. To bid defiance, To set at defiance, to defy; to disregard recklessly or contemptuously. Locke.
