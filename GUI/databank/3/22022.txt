obelisk
1. An upright, four-sided pillar, gradually tapering as it rises, and terminating in a pyramid called pyramidion. It is ordinarily monolithic. Egyptian obelisks are commonly covered with hieroglyphic writing from top to bottom. 2. (Print.)  A mark of reference; -- called also dagger [&dag;]. See Dagger, n., 2.\n\nTo mark or designate with an obelisk.
