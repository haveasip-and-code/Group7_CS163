zeal
1. Passionate ardor in the pursuit of anything; eagerness in favor of a person or cause; ardent and active interest; engagedness; enthusiasm; fervor. \"Ambition varnished o'er with zeal.\" Milton. \"Zeal, the blind conductor of the will.\" Dryden. \"Zeal's never-dying fire.\" Keble. I bear them record that they have a zeal of God, but not according to knowledge. Rom. x. 2. A zeal for liberty is sometimes an eagerness to subvert with little care what shall be established. Johnson. 2. A zealot. [Obs.] B. Jonson.\n\nTo be zealous. [Obs. & R.] Bacon.
