hulk
1. The body of a ship or decked vessel of any kind; esp., the body of an old vessel laid by as unfit for service. \"Some well-timbered hulk.\" Spenser. 2. A heavy ship of clumsy build. Skeat. 3. Anything bulky or unwieldly. Shak. Shear hulk, an old ship fitted with an apparatus to fix or take out the masts of a ship. -- The hulks, old or dismasted ships, formerly used as prisons. [Eng.] Dickens.\n\nTo take out the entrails of; to disembowel; as, to hulk a hare. [R.] Beau. & Fl.
