insnare
1. To catch in a snare; to entrap; to take by artificial means. \"Insnare a gudgeon.\" Fenton. 2. To take by wiles, stratagem, or deceit; to involve in difficulties or perplexities; to seduce by artifice; to inveigle; to allure; to entangle. The insnaring charms Of love's soft queen. Glover.
