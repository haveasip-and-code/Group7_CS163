hank
1. A parcel consisting of two or more skeins of yarn or thread tied together. 2. A rope or withe for fastening a gate. [Prov. Eng.] 3. Hold; influence. When the devil hath got such a hank over him. Bp. Sanderson. 4. (Naut.)  A ring or eye of rope, wood, or iron, attached to the edge of a sail and running on a stay.\n\n1. Etym: [OE. hanken.]  To fasten with a rope, as a gate. [Prov. Eng.] Wright. 2. To form into hanks.
