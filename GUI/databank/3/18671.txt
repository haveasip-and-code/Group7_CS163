snood
1. The fillet which binds the hair of a young unmarried woman, and is emblematic of her maiden character. [Scot.] And seldom was a snood amid Such wild, luxuriant ringlets hid. Sir W. Scott. 2. A short line (often of horsehair) connecting a fishing line with the hook; a snell; a leader.\n\nTo bind or braid up, as the hair, with a snood. [Scot.]
