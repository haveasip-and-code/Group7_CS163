scot
A name for a horse. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\nA native or inhabitant of Scotland; a Scotsman, or Scotchman.\n\nA portion of money assessed or paid; a tax or contribution; a mulct; a fine; a shot. Scot and lot, formerly, a parish assessment laid on subjects according to their ability. [Eng.] Cowell. Now, a phrase for obligations of every kind regarded collectivelly. Experienced men of the world know very well that it is best to pay scot and lot as they go along. Emerson.
