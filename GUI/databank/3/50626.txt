unmanned
1. Etym: [Properly p. p. of unman.]  Deprived of manly qualities; deficient in vigor, strength, courage, etc.; weak; effeminate. 2. Etym: [Pref. un- not + man + -ed.] (Falconry)  Not tamed; not made familiar with, or subject to, man; -- also used figuratively. [Obs.] Hood my unmanned blood bating in my cheeks With thy black mantle. Shak. 3. Etym: [Pref. un- not + manned.]  Not furnished with men; as, an unmanned ship.
