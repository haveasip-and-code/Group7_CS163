overture
1. An opening or aperture; a recess; a recess; a chamber. [Obs.] Spenser. \"The cave's inmost overture.\" Chapman. 2. Disclosure; discovery; revelation. [Obs.] It was he That made the overture of thy treasons to us. Shak. 3. A proposal; an offer; a proposition formally submitted for consideration, acceptance, or rejection. \"The great overture of the gospel.\" Barrow. 4. (Mus.)  A composition, for a full orchestra, designed as an introduction to an oratorio, opera, or ballet, or as an independent piece; -- called in the latter case a concert overture.\n\nTo make an overture to; as, to overture a religious body on some subject.
