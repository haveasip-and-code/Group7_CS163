impropriate
1. To appropriate to one's self; to assume. [Obs.] To impropriate the thanks to himself. Bacon. 2. (Eng. Eccl. Law)  To place the profits of (ecclesiastical property) in the hands of a layman for care and disbursement.\n\nTo become an impropriator. [R.]\n\nPut into the hands of a layman; impropriated.
