dent
1. A stroke; a blow. [Obs.] \"That dent of thunder.\" Chaucer. 2. A slight depression, or small notch or hollow, made by a blow or by pressure; an indentation. A blow that would have made a dent in a pound of butter. De Quincey.\n\nTo make a dent upon; to indent. The houses dented with bullets. Macaulay.\n\nA tooth, as of a card, a gear wheel, etc. Knight.
