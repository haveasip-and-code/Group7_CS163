coak
See Coke, n.\n\n1. (Carp.)  A kind of tenon connecting the face of a scarfed timber with the face of another timber, or a dowel or pin of hard wood or iron uniting timbers. [Also spelt coag.] 2. A metallic bushing or strengthening piece in the center of a wooden block sheve.\n\nTo unite, as timbers, by means of tenons or dowels in the edges or face. Totten.
