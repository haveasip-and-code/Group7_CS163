onward
1. Moving in a forward direction; tending toward a contemplated or desirable end; forward; as, an onward course, progress, etc. 2. Advanced in a forward direction or toward an end. Within a while, Philoxenus came to see how onward the fruits were of his friend's labor. Sir P. Sidney.\n\nToward a point before or in front; forward; progressively; as, to move onward. Not one looks backward, onward still he goes. Pope.
