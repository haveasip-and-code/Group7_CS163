anagram
Literally, the letters of a word read backwards, but in its usual wider sense, the change or one word or phrase into another by the transposition of its letters. Thus Galenus becomes angelus; William Noy (attorney-general to Charles I., and a laborious man) may be turned into I moyl in law.\n\nTo anagrammatize. Some of these anagramed his name, Benlowes, into Benevolus. Warburton.
