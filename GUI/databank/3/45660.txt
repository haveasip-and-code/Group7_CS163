rowel
1. The little wheel of a spur, with sharp points. With sounding whip, and rowels dyed in blood. Cowper. 2. A little flat ring or wheel on horses' bits. The iron rowels into frothy foam he bit. Spenser. 3. (Far.)  A roll of hair, silk, etc., passed through the flesh of horses, answering to a seton in human surgery.\n\nTo insert a rowel, or roll of hair or silk, into (as the flesh of a horse). Mortimer.
