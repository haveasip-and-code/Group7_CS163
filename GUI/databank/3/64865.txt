chit
1. The embryo or the growing bud of a plant; a shoot; a sprout; as, the chits of Indian corn or of potatoes. 2. A child or babe; as, a forward chit; also, a young, small, or insignificant person or animal. A little chit of a woman. Thackeray. 3. An excrescence on the body, as a wart. [Obs.] 4. A small tool used in cleaving laths. Knight.\n\nTo shoot out; to sprout. I have known barley chit in seven hours after it had been thrown forth. Mortimer.\n\nChideth. [Obs.] Chaucer.
