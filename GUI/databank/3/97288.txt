errant
1. Wandering; deviating from an appointed course, or from a direct path; roving. Seven planets or errant stars in the lower orbs of heaven. Sir T. Browne. 2. Notorious; notoriously bad; downright; arrant. Would make me an errant fool. B. Jonson. 3. (Eng. Law)  Journeying; itinerant; -- formerly applied to judges who went on circuit and to bailiffs at large. Mozley & W.\n\nOne who wanders about. [Obs.] Fuller.
