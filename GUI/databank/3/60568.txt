mull
A thin, soft kind of muslin.\n\n1. A promontory; as, the Mull of Cantyre. [Scot.] 2. A snuffbox made of the small end of a horn.\n\nDirt; rubbish. [Obs.] Gower.\n\nTo powder; to pulverize. [Prov. Eng.]\n\nTo work (over) mentally; to cogitate; to ruminate; -- usually with over; as, to mull over a thought or a problem. [Colloq. U.S.]\n\nAn inferior kind of madder prepared from the smaller roots or the peelings and refuse of the larger.\n\n1. To heat, sweeten, and enrich with spices; as, to mull wine. New cider, mulled with ginger warm. Gay. 2. To dispirit or deaden; to dull or blunt. Shak.
