diverse
1. Different; unlike; dissimilar; distinct; separate. The word . . . is used in a sense very diverse from its original import. J. Edwards. Our roads are diverse: farewell, love! said she. R. Browning. 2. Capable of various forms; multiform. Eloquence is a great and diverse thing. B. Jonson.\n\nIn different directions; diversely.\n\nTo turn aside. [Obs.] The redcross knight diverst, but forth rode Britomart. Spenser.
