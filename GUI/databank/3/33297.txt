cavil
To raise captious and frivolous objections; to find fault without good reason. You do not well in obstinacy To cavil in the course of this contract. Shak.\n\nTo cavil at. [Obs.] Milton.\n\nA captious or frivolous objection. All the cavils of prejudice and unbelief. Shak.\n\nOne who cavils. Cavilers at the style of the Scriptures. Boyle.
