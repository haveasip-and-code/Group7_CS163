soothfast
Firmly fixed in, or founded upon, the thruth; true; genuine; real; also, truthful; faithful. [Archaic] -- Sooth\"fast`ness, n. [Archaic] \"In very soothfastness.\" Chaucer. Why do not you . . . bear leal and soothfast evidence in her behalf, as ye may with a clear conscience! Sir W. Scott.\n\nSoothly; really; in fact. [Archaic] I care not if the pomps you show Be what they soothfast appear. Emerson.
