palter
1. To haggle. [Obs.] Cotgrave. 2. To act in insincere or deceitful manner; to play false; to equivocate; to shift; to dodge; to trifle. Romans, that have spoke the word, And will not palter. Shak. Who never sold the truth to serve the hour, Nor paltered with eternal God for power. Tennyson. 3. To babble; to chatter. [Obs.]\n\nTo trifle with; to waste; to squander in paltry ways or on worthless things. [Obs.] \"Palter out your time in the penal statutes.\" Beau. & Fl.
