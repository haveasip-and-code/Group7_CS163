pharisaic
1. Of or pertaining to the Pharisees; resembling the Pharisees. \"The Pharisaic sect among the Jews.\" Cudworth. 2. Hence: Addicted to external forms and ceremonies; making a show of religion without the spirit of it; ceremonial; formal; hypocritical; self-righteous. \"Excess of outward and pharisaical holiness. \" Bacon. \"Pharisaical ostentation.\" Macaulay. -- Phar`i*sa\"ic*al*ly, adv. -- Phar`i*sa\"ic*al*ness, n.
