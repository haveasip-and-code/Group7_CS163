circumflex
1. A wave of the voice embracing both a rise and fall or a fall and a rise on the same a syllable. Walker. 2. A character, or accent, denoting in Greek a rise and of the voice on the same long syllable, marked thus [~ or Accent, n., 2.\n\nTo mark or pronounce with a circumflex. Walker.\n\n1. Moving or turning round; circuitous. [R.] Swift. 2. (Anat.)  Curved circularly; -- applied to several arteries of the hip and thigh, to arteries, veins, and a nerve of the shoulder, and to other parts.
