sycophant
1. An informer; a talebearer. [Obs.] \"Accusing sycophants, of all men, did best sort to his nature.\" Sir P. Sidney. 2. A base parasite; a mean or servile flatterer; especially, a flatterer of princes and great men. A sycophant will everything admire: Each verse, each sentence, sets his soul on fire. Dryden.\n\n1. To inform against; hence, to calumniate. [Obs.] Sycophanting and misnaming the work of his adversary. Milton. 2. To play the sycophant toward; to flatter obsequiously.\n\nTo play the sycophant.
