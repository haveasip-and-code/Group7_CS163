vengeance
1. Punishment inflicted in return for an injury or an offense; retribution; -- often, in a bad sense, passionate or unrestrained revenge. To me belongeth vengeance and recompense. Deut. xxxii. 35. To execute fierce vengeance on his foes. Milton. 2. Harm; mischief. [Obs.] Shak. What a vengeance, or What the vengeance, what! -- emphatically. [Obs.] \"But what a vengeance makes thee fly!\" Hudibras. \"What the vengeance! Could he not speak 'em fair\" Shak. -- With a vengeance, with great violence; as, to strike with a vengeance. [Colloq.]
