assemble
To collect into one place or body; to bring or call together; to convene; to congregate. Thither he assembled all his train. Milton. All the men of Israel assembled themselves. 1 Kings viii. 2.\n\nTo meet or come together, as a number of individuals; to convene; to congregate. Dryden. The Parliament assembled in November. W. Massey.\n\nTo liken; to compare. [Obs.] Bribes may be assembled to pitch. Latimer.
