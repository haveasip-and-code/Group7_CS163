path
1. A trodden way; a footway. The dewy paths of meadows we will tread. Dryden. 2. A way, course, or track, in which anything moves or has moved; route; passage; an established way; as, the path of a meteor, of a caravan, of a storm, of a pestilence. Also used figuratively, of a course of life or action. All the paths of the Lord are mercy and truth. Ps. xxv. 10. The paths of glory lead but to the grave. Gray.\n\nTo make a path in, or on (something), or for (some one). [R.] \"Pathing young Henry's unadvised ways.\" Drayton.\n\nTo walk or go. [R.] Shak.
