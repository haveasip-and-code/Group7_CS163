crunch
1. To chew with force and noise; to craunch. And their white tusks crunched o'er the whiter skull. Byron. 2. To grind or press with violence and noise. The ship crunched through the ice. Kane. 3. To emit a grinding or craunching noise. The crunching and ratting of the loose stones. H. James.\n\nTo crush with the teeth; to chew with a grinding noise; to craunch; as, to crunch a biscuit.
