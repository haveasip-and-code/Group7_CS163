skulk
To hide, or get out of the way, in a sneaking manner; to lie close, or to move in a furtive way; to lurk. \"Want skulks in holes and crevices.\" W. C. Bryant. Discovered and defeated of your prey, You skulked behind the fence, and sneaked away. Dryden.\n\nA number of foxes together. Wright.\n\nOne who, or that which, skulks.
