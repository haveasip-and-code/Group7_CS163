fettle
1. To repair; to prepare; to put in order. [Prov. Eng.] Carlyle. 2. (Metal.)  To cover or line with a mixture of ore, cinders, etc., as the hearth of a puddling furnace.\n\nTo make preparations; to put things in order; to do trifling business. [Prov. Eng.] Bp. Hall.\n\nThe act of fettling. [Prov. Eng.] Wright. In fine fettle, in good spirits.
