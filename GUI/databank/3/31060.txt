crake
1. To cry out harshly and loudly, like the bird called crake. 2. To boast; to speak loudly and boastfully. [Obs.] Each man may crake of that which was his own. Mir. for Mag.\n\nA boast. See Crack, n. [Obs.] Spenser.\n\nAny species or rail of the genera Crex and Porzana; -- so called from its singular cry. See Corncrake.
