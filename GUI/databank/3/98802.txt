dribble
1. To fall in drops or small drops, or in a quick succession of drops; as, water dribbles from the eaves. 2. To slaver, as a child or an idiot; to drivel. 3. To fall weakly and slowly. [Obs.] \"The dribbling dart of love.\" Shak. (Meas. for Meas. , i. 3, 2). [Perhaps an error for dribbing.]\n\nTo let fall in drops. Let the cook . . . dribble it all the way upstairs. Swift.\n\nA drizzling shower; a falling or leaking in drops. [Colloq.]
