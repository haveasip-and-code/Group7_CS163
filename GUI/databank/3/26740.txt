swinge
See Singe. [Obs.] Spenser.\n\n1. To beat soundly; to whip; to chastise; to punish. I had swinged him soundly. Shak. And swinges his own vices in his son. C. Dryden. 2. To move as a lash; to lash. [Obs.] Swinges the scaly horror of his folded tail. Milton.\n\n1. The sweep of anything in motion; a swinging blow; a swing. [Obs.] Waller. 2. Power; sway; influence. [Obs.]
