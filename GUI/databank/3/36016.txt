outlook
1. To face down; to outstare. To outlook conquest, and to win renown. Shak. 2. To inspect throughly; to select. [Obs.] Cotton.\n\n1. The act of looking out; watch. 2. One who looks out; also, the place from which one looks out; a watchower. Lyon Playfair. 3. The view obtained by one looking out; scope of vision; prospect; sight; appearance. Applause Which owes to man's short outlook all its charms. Young.
