expletive
Filling up; hence, added merely for the purpose of filling up; superfluous. \"Expletive imagery.\" Hallam. Expletive phrases to plump his speech. Barrow.\n\nA word, letter, or syllable not necessary to the sense, but inserted to fill a vacancy; an oath. While explectives their feeble aid to join, And ten low words oft creep in one dull line. Pope.
