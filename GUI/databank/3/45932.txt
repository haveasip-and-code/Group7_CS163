absence
1. A state of being absent or withdrawn from a place or from companionship; -- opposed to presence. Not as in my presence only, but now much more in my absence. Phil. ii. 12. 2. Want; destitution; withdrawal. \"In the absence of conventional law.\" Kent. 3. Inattention to things present; abstraction (of mind); as, absence of mind. \"Reflecting on the little absences and distractions of mankind.\" Addison. To conquer that abstraction which is called absence. Landor.
