winch
To wince; to shrink; to kick with impatience or uneasiness.\n\nA kick, as of a beast, from impatience or uneasiness. Shelton.\n\n1. A crank with a handle, for giving motion to a machine, a grindstone, etc. 2. An instrument with which to turn or strain something forcibly. 3. An axle or drum turned by a crank with a handle, or by power, for raising weights, as from the hold of a ship, from mines, etc.; a windlass. 4. A wince.
