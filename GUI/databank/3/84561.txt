jargon
Confused, unintelligible language; gibberish; hence, an artificial idiom or dialect; cant language; slang. \"A barbarous jargon.\" Macaulay. \"All jargon of the schools.\" Prior. The jargon which serves the traffickers. Johnson.\n\nTo utter jargon; to emit confused or unintelligible sounds; to talk unintelligibly, or in a harsh and noisy manner. The noisy jay, Jargoning like a foreigner at his food. Longfellow.\n\nA variety of zircon. See Zircon.
