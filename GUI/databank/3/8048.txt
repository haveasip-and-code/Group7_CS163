prostitute
1. To offer, as a woman, to a lewd use; to give up to lewdness for hire. \"Do not prostitute thy daughter.\" Lev. xix. 29. 2. To devote to base or unworthy purposes; to give up to low or indiscriminate use; as, to prostitute talents; to prostitute official powers. Milton.\n\nOpenly given up to lewdness; devoted to base or infamous purposes. Made bold by want, and prostitute for bread. Prior\n\n1. A woman giver to indiscriminate lewdness; a strumpet; a harlot. 2. A base hireling; a mercenary; one who offers himself to infamous employments for hire. No hireling she, no prostitute to praise. Pope.
