rapt
imp. & p. p. of Rap, to snatch away.\n\n1. Snatched away; hurried away or along. Waters rapt with whirling away. Spenser. 2. Transported with love, admiration, delight, etc.; enraptured. \"The rapt musician.\" Longfellow. 3. Wholly absorbed or engrossed, as in work or meditation. \"Rapt in secret studies.\" Shak.\n\n1. An ecstasy; a trance. [Obs.] Bp. Morton. 2. Rapidity. [Obs.] Sir T. Browne.\n\n1. To transport or ravish. [Obs.] Drayton. 2. To carry away by force. [Obs.] Daniel.
