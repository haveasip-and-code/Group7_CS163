clang
To strike together so as to produce a ringing metallic sound. The fierce Caretes . . . clanged their sounding arms. Prior.\n\nTo give out a clang; to resound. \"Clanging hoofs.\" Tennyson.\n\n1. A loud, ringing sound, like that made by metallic substances when clanged or struck together. The broadsword's deadly clang, As if a thousand anvils rang. Sir W. Scott. 2. (Mus.)  Qualyty of tone.
