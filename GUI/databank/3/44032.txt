The highest part of anything; the upper end, edge, or extremity; the upper side or surface; summit; apex; vertex; cover; lid; as, the top of a spire; the top of a house; the top of a mountain; the top of the ground. The star that bids the shepherd fold, Now the top of heaven doth hold. Milton. 2. The utmost degree; the acme; the summit. The top of my ambition is to contribute to that work. Pope. 3. The buds . . . are called heads, or tops, as cabbageheads. I. Watts. 7. (Naut.)  A platform surrounding the head of the lower mast and projecting on all sudes.
