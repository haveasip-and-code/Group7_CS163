frenzy
Any violent agitation of the mind approaching to distraction; violent and temporary derangement of the mental faculties; madness; rage. All else is towering frenzy and distraction. Addison. The poet's eye in a fine frenzy rolling. Shak. Syn. -- Insanity; lunacy; madness; derangment; alienation; aberration; delirium. See Insanity.\n\nMad; frantic. [R.] They thought that some frenzy distemper had got into his head. Bunyan.\n\nTo affect with frenzy; to drive to madness [R.] \"Frenzying anguish.\" Southey.
