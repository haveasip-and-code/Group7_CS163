abode
of Abide.\n\n1. Act of waiting; delay. [Obs.] Shak. And with her fled away without abode. Spenser. 2. Stay or continuance in a place; sojourn. He waxeth at your abode here. Fielding. 3. Place of continuance, or where one dwells; abiding place; residence; a dwelling; a habitation. Come, let me lead you to our poor abode. Wordsworth.\n\nAn omen. [Obs.] High-thundering Juno's husband stirs my spirit with true abodes. Chapman.\n\nTo bode; to foreshow. [Obs.] Shak.\n\nTo be ominous. [Obs.] Dryden.
