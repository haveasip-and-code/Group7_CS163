flippant
1. Of smooth, fluent, and rapid speech; speaking with ease and rapidity; having a voluble tongue; talkative. It becometh good men, in such cases, to be flippant and free in their speech. Barrow. 2. Speaking fluently and confidently, without knowledge or consideration; empty; trifling; inconsederate; pert; petulant. \"Flippant epilogous.\" Thomson. To put flippant scorn to the blush. I. Taylor. A sort of flippant, vain discourse. Burke.\n\nA flippant person. [R.] Tennyson.
