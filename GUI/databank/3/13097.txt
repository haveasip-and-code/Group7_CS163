changeling
1. One who, or that which, is left or taken in the place of another, as a child exchanged by fairies. Such, men do changelings call, so changed by fairies' theft. Spenser. The changeling [a substituted writing] never known. Shak. 2. A simpleton; an idiot. Macaulay. Changelings and fools of heaven, and thence shut out. Wildly we roam in discontent about. Dryden. 3. One apt to change; a waverer. \"Fickle changelings.\" Shak.\n\n1. Taken or left in place of another; changed. \"A little changeling boy.\" Shak. 2. Given to change; inconstant. [Obs.] Some are so studiously changeling. Boyle.
