informer
1. One who informs, animates, or inspires. [Obs.] Thomson. Nature, informer of the poet's art. Pope. 2. One who informs, or imparts knowledge or news. 3. (Law)  One who informs a magistrate of violations of law; one who informs against another for violation of some law or penal statute. Common informer (Law), one who habitually gives information of the violation of penal statutes, with a view to a prosecution therefor. Bouvier. Wharton.
