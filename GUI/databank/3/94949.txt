rebus
1. A mode of expressing words and phrases by pictures of objects whose names resemble those words, or the syllables of which they are composed; enigmatical representation of words by figures; hence, a peculiar form of riddle made up of such representations. Note: A gallant, in love with a woman named Rose Hill, had, embroidered on his gown, a rose, a hill, an eye, a loaf, and a well, signifying, Rose Hill I love well. 2. (Her.)  A pictorial suggestion on a coat of arms of the name of the person to whom it belongs. See Canting arms, under Canting.\n\nTo mark or indicate by a rebus. He [John Morton] had a fair library rebused with More in text and Tun under it. Fuller.
