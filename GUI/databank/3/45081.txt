trigon
1. A figure having three angles; a triangle. 2. (Astrol.) (a) A division consisting of three signs. (b) Trine, an aspect of two planets distant 120 degrees from each other. Hutton. 3. (Gr. & Rom. Antiq.) (a) A kind of triangular lyre or harp. (b) A kind of game at ball played by three persons standing at the angular points of a triangle.\n\nThe cutting region of the crown of an upper molar, usually the anterior part. That of a lower molar is the Tri\"go*nid.
