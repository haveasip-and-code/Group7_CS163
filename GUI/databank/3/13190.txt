prejudicate
1. Formed before due examination. \"Ignorance and prejudicate opinions.\" Jer. Taylor. 2. Biased by opinions formed prematurely; prejudiced. \"Prejudicate readers.\" Sir T. Browne.\n\nTo determine beforehand, especially to disadvantage; to prejudge. Our dearest friend Prejudicates the business. Shak.\n\nTo prejudge. Sir P. Sidney.
