lest
To listen. [Obs.] Chaucer. Spenser.\n\nLust; desire; pleasure. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\nLast; least. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\n1. For Love not sleep, lest thou come to poverty. Prov. xx. 18. Wherefore let him that thinketh he standeth he standeth take heed lest he fall. I Cor. x. 12. 2. That (without the negative particle); -- after certain expressions denoting fear or apprehension. I feared Lest I might anger thee. Shak.
