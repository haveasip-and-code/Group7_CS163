tenter
1. One who takes care of, or tends, machines in a factory; a kind of assistant foreman. 2. (Mach.)  A kind of governor.\n\nA machine or frame for stretching cloth by means of hooks, called tenter-hooks, so that it may dry even and square. Tenter ground, a place where tenters are erected. -- Tenter-hook, a sharp, hooked nail used for fastening cloth on a tenter. -- To be on the tenters, or on the tenter-hooks, to be on the stretch; to be in distress, uneasiness, or suspense. Hudibras.\n\nTo admit extension. Woolen cloth will tenter, linen scarcely. Bacon.\n\nTo hang or stretch on, or as on, tenters.
