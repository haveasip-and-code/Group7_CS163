distinct
1. Distinguished; having the difference marked; separated by a visible sign; marked out; specified. [Obs.] Wherever thus created -- for no place Is yet distinct by name. Milton. 2. Marked; variegated. [Obs.] The which [place] was dight With divers flowers distinct with rare delight. Spenser. 3. Separate in place; not conjunct; not united by growth or otherwise; -- with from. The intention was that the two armies which marched out together should afterward be distinct. Clarendon. 4. Not identical; different; individual. To offend, and judge, are distinct offices. Shak. 5. So separated as not to be confounded with any other thing; not liable to be misunderstood; not confused; well-defined; clear; as, we have a distinct or indistinct view of a prospect. Relation more particular and distinct. Milton. Syn. -- Separate; unconnected; disjoined; different; clear; plain; conspicuous; obvious.\n\nTo distinguish. [Obs.] Rom. of R.
