parget
1. To coat with parget; to plaster, as walls, or the interior of flues; as, to parget the outside of their houses. Sir T. Herbert. The pargeted ceiling with pendants. R. L. Stevenson. 2. To paint; to cover over. [Obs.]\n\n1. To lay on plaster. 2. To paint, as the face. [Obs.] B. Jonson.\n\n1. Gypsum or plaster stone. 2. Plaster, as for lining the interior of flues, or for stuccowork. Knight. 3. Paint, especially for the face. [Obs.] Drayton.
