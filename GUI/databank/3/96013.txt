chopping
Stout or plump; large. [Obs.] Fenton.\n\nShifting or changing suddenly, as the wind; also, having tumbling waves dashing against each other; as, a chopping sea.\n\nAct of cutting by strokes. Chopping block, a solid block of wood on which butchers and others chop meat, etc. -- Chopping knife, a knife for chopping or mincing meat, vegetables, etc.; -- usually with a handle at the back of the blade instead of at the end.
