wisp
1. A small bundle, as of straw or other like substance. In a small basket, on a wisp of hay. Dryden. 2. A whisk, or small broom. 3. A Will-o'-the-wisp; an ignis fatuus. The wisp that flickers where no foot can tread. Tennyson.\n\n1. To brush or dress, an with a wisp. 2. To rumple. [Prov. Eng.] Halliwell.
