impervious
Not pervious; not admitting of entrance or passage through; as, a substance impervious to water or air. This gulf impassable, impervious. Milton. The minds of these zealots were absolutely impervious. Macaulay. Syn. -- Impassable; pathless; impenetrable; imperviable; impermeable. -- Im*per\"vi*ous*ly, adv. -- Im*per\"vi*ous*ness, n.
