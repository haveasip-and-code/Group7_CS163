morne
Of or pertaining to the morn; morning. [Obs.] \"White as morne milk.\" Chaucer.\n\nA ring fitted upon the head of a lance to prevent wounding an adversary in titling.\n\nWithout teeth, tongue, or claws; -- said of a lion represented heraldically.\n\n1. The first or early part of the day, variously understood as the earliest hours of light, the time near sunrise; the time from midnight to noon, from rising to noon, etc. 2. The first or early part; as, the morning of life. 3. The goddess Aurora. [Poetic] Shak.
