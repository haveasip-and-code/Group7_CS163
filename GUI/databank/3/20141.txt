ooze
1. Soft mud or slime; earth so wet as to flow gently, or easily yield to pressure. \"My son i' the ooze is bedded.\" Shak. 2. Soft flow; spring. Prior. 3. The liquor of a tan vat.\n\n1. To flow gently; to percolate, as a liquid through the pores of a substance or through small openings. The latent rill, scare oozing through the grass. Thomson. 2. Fig.: To leak (out) or escape slowly; as, the secret oozed out; his courage oozed out.\n\nTo cause to ooze. Alex. Smith.
