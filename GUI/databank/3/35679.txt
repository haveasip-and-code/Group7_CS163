pun
To pound. [Obs.] He would pun thee into shivers with his fist. Shak.\n\nA play on words which have the same sound but different meanings; an expression in which two different applications of a word present an odd or ludicrous idea; a kind of quibble or equivocation. Addison. A better put on this word was made on the Beggar's Opera, which, it was said, made Gay rich, and Rich gay. Walpole.\n\nTo make puns, or a pun; to use a word in a double sense, especially when the contrast of ideas is ludicrous; to play upon words; to quibble. Dryden.\n\nTo persuade or affect by a pun. Addison.
