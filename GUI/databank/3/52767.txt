dibble
A pointed implement used to make holes in the ground in which no set out plants or to plant seeds.\n\nTo dib or dip frequently, as in angling. Walton.\n\n1. To plant with a dibble; to make holes in (soil) with a dibble, for planting. 2. To make holes or indentations in, as if with a dibble. The clayey soil around it was dibbled thick at the time by the tiny hoofs of sheep. H. Miller.
