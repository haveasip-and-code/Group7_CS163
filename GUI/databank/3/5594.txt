commensurate
1. To reduce to a common measure. Sir T. Browne. 2. To proportionate; to adjust. T. Puller\n\n1. Having a common measure; commensurable; reducible to a common measure; as, commensurate quantities. 2. Equal in measure or extent; proportionate. Those who are persuaded that they shall continue forever, can not choose but aspire after a hapiness commensurate to their duration. Tillotson.
