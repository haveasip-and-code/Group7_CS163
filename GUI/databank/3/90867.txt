frontier
1. That part of a country which fronts or faces another country or an unsettled region; the marches; the border, confine, or extreme part of a country, bordering on another country; the border of the settled and cultivated part of a country; as, the frontier of civilization. 2. (Fort.)  An outwork. [Obs.] Palisadoes, frontiers, parapets. Shak.\n\n1. Lying on the exterior part; bordering; conterminous; as, a frontier town. 2. Of or relating to a frontier. \"Frontier experience.\" W. Irving.\n\nTo constitute or form a frontier; to have a frontier; -- with on. [Obs.] Sir W. Temple.
