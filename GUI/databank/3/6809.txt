whelp
1. One of the young of a dog or a beast of prey; a puppy; a cub; as, a lion's whelps. \"A bear robbed of her whelps.\" 2 Sam. xvii. 8. 2. A child; a youth; -- jocosely or in contempt. That awkward whelp with his money bags would have made his entrance. Addison. 3. (Naut.)  One of the longitudinal ribs or ridges on the barrel of a capstan or a windless; -- usually in the plural; as, the whelps of a windlass. 4. One of the teeth of a sprocket wheel.\n\nTo bring forth young; -- said of the female of the dog and some beasts of prey.\n\nTo bring forth, as cubs or young; to give birth to. Unless she had whelped it herself, she could not have loved a thing better. B. Jonson. Did thy foul fancy whelp so black a scheme Young.
