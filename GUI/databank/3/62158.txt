ledge
1. A shelf on which articles may be laid; also, that which resembles such a shelf in form or use, as a projecting ridge or part, or a molding or edge in joinery. 2. A shelf, ridge, or reef, of rocks. 3. A layer or stratum. The lowest ledge or row should be of stone. Sir H. Wotton. 4. (Mining)  A lode; a limited mass of rock bearing valuable mineral. 5. (Shipbuilding)  A piece of timber to support the deck, placed athwartship between beams.
