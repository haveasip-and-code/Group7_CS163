jut
1. To shoot out or forward; to project beyond the main body; as, the jutting part of a building. \"In jutting rock and curved shore.\" Wordsworth. It seems to jut out of the structure of the poem. Sir T. Browne. 2. To butt. [Obs.] \"The jutting steer.\" Mason.\n\n1. That which projects or juts; a projection. 2. A shove; a push. [Obs.] Udall.
