whimsey
1. A whim; a freak; a capricious notion, a fanciful or odd conceit. \"The whimsies of poets and painters.\" Ray. Men's folly, whimsies, and inconstancy. Swift. Mistaking the whimseys of a feverish brain for the calm revelation of truth. Bancroft. 2. (Mining)  A whim.\n\nTo fill with whimseys, or whims; to make fantastic; to craze. [R.] To have a man's brain whimsied with his wealth. J. Fletcher.
