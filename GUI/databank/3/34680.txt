tour
A tower. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\n1. A going round; a circuit; hence, a journey in a circuit; a prolonged circuitous journey; a comprehensive excursion; as, the tour of Europe; the tour of France or England. The bird of Jove stooped from his airy tour. Milton. 2. A turn; a revolution; as, the tours of the heavenly bodies. [Obs.] Blackmore. 3. (Mil.)  anything done successively, or by regular order; a turn; as, a tour of duty. Syn. -- Journey; excursion. See Journey.\n\nTo make a tourm; as, to tour throught a country. T. Hughes.
