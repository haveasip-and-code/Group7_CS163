hamper
A large basket, usually with a cover, used for the packing and carrying of articles; as, a hamper of wine; a clothes hamper; an oyster hamper, which contains two bushels.\n\nTo put in a hamper.\n\nTo put a hamper or fetter on; to shackle; to insnare; to inveigle; hence, to impede in motion or progress; to embarrass; to encumber. \"Hampered nerves.\" Blackmore. A lion hampered in a net. L'Estrange. They hamper and entangle our souls. Tillotson.\n\n1. A shackle; a fetter; anything which impedes. W. Browne. 2. (Naut.)  Articles ordinarily indispensable, but in the way at certain times. Ham. Nav. Encyc. Top hamper (Naut.), unnecessary spars and rigging kept aloft.
