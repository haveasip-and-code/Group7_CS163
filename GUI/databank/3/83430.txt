balcony
1. (Arch.)  A platform projecting from the wall of a building, usually resting on brackets or consoles, and inclosed by a parapet; as, a balcony in front of a window. Also, a projecting gallery in places of amusement; as, the balcony in a theater. 2. A projecting gallery once common at the stern of large ships. Note: \"The accent has shifted from the second to the first syllable within these twenty years.\" Smart (1836).
