maudlin
1. Tearful; easily moved to tears; exciting to tears; excessively sentimental; weak and silly. \"Maudlin eyes.\" Dryden. \"Maudlin eloquence.\" Roscommon. \"A maudlin poetess.\" Pope. \"Maudlin crowd.\" Southey. 2. Drunk, or somewhat drunk; fuddled; given to drunkenness. Maudlin Clarence in his malmsey butt. Byron.\n\nAn aromatic composite herb, the costmary; also, the South European Achillea Ageratum, a kind of yarrow.
