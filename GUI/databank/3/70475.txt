prig
To haggle about the price of a commodity; to bargain hard. [Prov. Eng. & Scot.]\n\n1. To cheapen. [Scot.] 2. Etym: [Perhaps orig., to ride off with. See Prick, v. t.]  To filch or steal; as, to prig a handkerchief. [Cant]\n\n1. A pert, conceited, pragmatical fellow. The queer prig of a doctor. Macaulay. 2. A thief; a filcher. [Cant] Shak.
