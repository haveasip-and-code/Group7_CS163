recall
1. To call back; to summon to return; as, to recall troops; to recall an ambassador. 2. To revoke; to annul by a subsequent act; to take back; to withdraw; as, to recall words, or a decree. Passed sentence may not be recall'd. Shak. 3. To call back to mind; to revive in memory; to recollect; to remember; as, to recall bygone days.\n\n1. A calling back; a revocation. 'T his done, and since 't is done, 't is past recall. Dryden. 2. (Mil.)  A call on the trumpet, bugle, or drum, by which soldiers are recalled from duty, labor, etc. Wilhelm.
