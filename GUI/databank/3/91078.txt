commendation
1. The act of commending; praise; favorable representation in words; recommendation. Need we . . . epistles of commendatiom 2 Cor. iii. 1. By the commendation of the great officers. Bacon. 2. That which is the ground of approbation or praise. Good nature is the most godlike commendation of a man. Dryden. 3. pl.  A message of affection or respect; compliments; greeting. [Obs.] Hark you, Margaret; No princely commendations to my king Shak.
