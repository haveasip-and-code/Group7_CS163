hoar
1. White, or grayish white: as, hoar frost; hoar cliffs. \"Hoar waters.\" Spenser. 2. Gray or white with age; hoary. Whose beard with age is hoar. Coleridge. Old trees with trunks all hoar. Byron. 3. Musty; moldy; stale. [Obs.] Shak.\n\nHoariness; antiquity. [R.] Covered with the awful hoar of innumerable ages. Burke.\n\nTo become moldy or musty. [Obs.] Shak.
