depaint
Painted. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\n1. To paint; to picture; hence, to describe; to delineate in words; to depict. [Obs.] And do unwilling worship to the saint That on his shield depainted he did see. Spenser. In few words shall see the nature of many memorable persons . . . depainted. Holland. 2. To mark with, or as with, color; to color. Silver drops her vermeil cheeks depaint. Fairfax.
