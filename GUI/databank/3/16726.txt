sunder
To disunite in almost any manner, either by rending, cutting, or breaking; to part; to put or keep apart; to separate; to divide; to sever; as, to sunder a rope; to sunder a limb; to sunder friends. It is sundered from the main land by a sandy plain. Carew.\n\nTo part; to separate. [R.] Shak.\n\nA separation into parts; a division or severance. In sunder, into parts. \"He breaketh the bow, and cutteth the spear in sunder.\" Ps. xlvi. 9.\n\nTo expose to the sun and wind. [Prov. Eng.] Halliwell.
