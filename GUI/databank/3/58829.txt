litigious
1. Inclined to judicial contest; given to the practice of contending in law; guarrelsome; contentious; fond of litigation. \" A pettifogging attorney or a litigious client.\" Macaulay. Soldiers find wars, and lawyers find out still Litigious men, who guarrels move. Donne. 2. Subject to contention; disputable; controvertible; debatable; doubtful; precarious. Shak. No fences, parted fields, nor marks, nor bounds, Distinguished acres of litigious grounds. Dryden. 3. Of or pertaining to legal disputes. Nor brothers cite to the litigious bar. Young.
