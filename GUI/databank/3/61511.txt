levant
Rising or having risen from rest; -- said of cattle. See Couchant and levant, under Couchant.\n\n1. The countries washed by the eastern part of the Mediterranean and its contiguous waters. 2. A levanter (the wind so called).\n\nEastern. [Obs.] Forth rush the levant and the ponent winds. Milton.\n\nTo run away from one's debts; to decamp. [Colloq. Eng.] Thackeray.
