funk
An offensive smell; a stench. [Low]\n\nTo envelop with an offensive smell or smoke. [Obs.] King.\n\n1. To emit an offensive smell; to stink. 2. To be frightened, and shrink back; to flinch; as, to funk at the edge of a precipice. [Colloq.] C. Kingsley. To funk out, to back out in a cowardly fashion. [Colloq.] To funk right out o' political strife. Lowell (Biglow Papers).\n\nA shrinking back through fear. [Colloq.] \"The horrid panic, or funk (as the men of Eton call it).\" De Quincey.
