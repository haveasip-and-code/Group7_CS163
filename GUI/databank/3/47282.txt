stare
The starling. [Obs.]\n\n1. To look with fixed eyes wide open, as through fear, wonder, surprise, impudence, etc.; to fasten an earnest and prolonged gaze on some object. For ever upon the ground I see thee stare. Chaucer. Look not big, nor stamp, nor stare, nor fret. Shak. 2. To be very conspicuous on account of size, prominence, color, or brilliancy; as, staring windows or colors. 3. To stand out; to project; to bristle. [Obs.] Makest my blood cold, and my hair to stare. Shak. Take off all the staring straws and jags in the hive. Mortimer. Syn. -- To gaze; to look earnestly. See Gaze.\n\nTo look earnestly at; to gaze at. I will stare him out of his wits. Shak. To stare in the face, to be before the eyes, or to be undeniably evident. \"The law . . . stares them in the face whilst they are breaking it.\" Locke.\n\nThe act of staring; a fixed look with eyes wide open. \"A dull and stupid stare.\" Churchill.
