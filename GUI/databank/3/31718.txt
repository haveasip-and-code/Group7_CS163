puncheon
1. A figured stamp, die, or punch, used by goldsmiths, cutlers, etc. 2. (Carp.)  A short, upright piece of timber in framing; a short post; an intermediate stud. Oxf. Gloss. 3. A split log or heavy slab with the face smoothed; as, a floor made of puncheons. [U.S.] Bartlett. 4. Etym: [F. poinçon, perh. the same as poinçon an awl.]  A cask containing, sometimes 84, sometimes 120, gallons.
