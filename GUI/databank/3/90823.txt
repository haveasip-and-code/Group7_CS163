behoove
To be necessary for; to be fit for; to be meet for, with respect to necessity, duty, or convenience; -- mostly used impersonally. And thus it behooved Christ to suffer. Luke xxiv. 46. [Also written behove.]\n\nTo be necessary, fit, or suitable; to befit; to belong as due. Chaucer.\n\nAdvantage; behoof. [Obs.] It shall not be to his behoove. Gower.
