methodist
1. One who observes method. [Obs.] 2. One of an ancient school of physicians who rejected observation and founded their practice on reasoning and theory. Sir W. Hamilton. 3. (Theol.)  One of a sect of Christians, the outgrowth of a small association called the \"Holy Club,\" formed at Oxford University, A.D. 1729, of which the most conspicuous members were John Wesley and his brother Charles; -- originally so called from the methodical strictness of members of the club in all religious duties. 4. A person of strict piety; one who lives in the exact observance of religious duties; -- sometimes so called in contempt or ridicule.\n\nOf or pertaining to the sect of Methodists; as, Methodist hymns; a Methodist elder.
