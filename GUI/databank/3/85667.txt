den
1. A small cavern or hollow place in the side of a hill, or among rocks; esp., a cave used by a wild beast for shelter or concealment; as, a lion's den; a den of robbers. 2. A squalid place of resort; a wretched dwelling place; a haunt; as, a den of vice. \"Those squalid dens, which are the reproach of great capitals.\" Addison. 3. Any snug or close retreat where one goes to be alone. [Colloq.] 4. Etym: [AS. denu.]  A narrow glen; a ravine; a dell. [Old Eng. & Scotch] Shak.\n\nTo live in, or as in, a den. The sluggish salvages that den below. G. Fletcher.
