wedlock
1. The ceremony, or the state, of marriage; matrimony. \"That blissful yoke . . . that men clepeth [call] spousal, or wedlock.\" Chaucer. For what is wedlock forced but a hell, An age of discord or continual strife Shak. 2. A wife; a married woman. [Obs.] B. Jonson. Syn. -- See Marriage.\n\nTo marry; to unite in marriage; to wed. [R.] \"Man thus wedlocked.\" Milton.
