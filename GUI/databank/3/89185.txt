petulancy
The quality or state of being petulant; temporary peevishness; pettishness; capricious ill humor. \"The petulancy of our words.\" B. Jonson. Like pride in some, and like petulance in others. Clarendon. The lowering eye, the petulance, the frown. Cowper. Syn. -- Petulance, Peevishness. -- Peevishness implies the permanence of a sour, fretful temper; petulance implies temporary or capricious irritation.
