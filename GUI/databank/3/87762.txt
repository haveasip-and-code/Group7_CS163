quiddity
1. The essence, nature, or distinctive peculiarity, of a thing; that which answers the question, Quid est or, What is it \" The degree of nullity and quiddity.\" Bacon. The quiddity or characteristic difference of poetry as distinguished from prose. De Quincey. 2. A trifling nicety; a cavil; a quibble. We laugh at the quiddities of those writers now. Coleridge.
