hurt
(a) A band on a trip-hammer helve, bearing the trunnions. (b) A husk. See Husk, 2.\n\n1. To cause physical pain to; to do bodily harm to; to wound or bruise painfully. The hurt lion groans within his den. Dryden. 2. To impar the value, usefulness, beauty, or pleasure of; to damage; to injure; to harm. Virtue may be assailed, but never hurt. Milton. 3. To wound the feelings of; to cause mental pain to; to offend in honor or self-respect; to annoy; to grieve. \"I am angry and hurt.\" Thackeray.
