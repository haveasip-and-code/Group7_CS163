rendezvous
1. A place appointed for a meeting, or at which persons customarily meet. An inn, the free rendezvous of all travelers. Sir W. Scott. 2. Especially, the appointed place for troops, or for the ships of a fleet, to assemble; also, a place for enlistment. The king appointed his whole army to be drawn together to a rendezvous at Marlborough. Clarendon. 3. A meeting by appointment. Sprat. 4. Retreat; refuge. [Obs.] Shak.\n\nTo assemble or meet at a particular place.\n\nTo bring together at a certain place; to cause to be assembled. Echard.
