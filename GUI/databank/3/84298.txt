cade
Bred by hand; domesticated; petted. He brought his cade lamb with him. Sheldon.\n\nTo bring up or nourish by hand, or with tenderness; to coddle; to tame. [Obs.] Johnson.\n\nA barrel or cask, as of fish. \"A cade of herrings.\" Shak. A cade of herrings is 500, of sprats 1,000. Jacob, Law Dict.\n\nA species of juniper (Juniperus Oxycedrus) of Mediterranean countries. Oil of cade, a thick, black, tarry liquid, obtained by destructive distillation of the inner wood of the cade. It is used as a local application in skin diseases.
