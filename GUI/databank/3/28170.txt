rebut
1. To drive or beat back; to repulse. Who him, recount'ring fierce, as hawk in flight, Perforce rebutted back. Spenser. 2. (Law)  To contradict, meet, or oppose by argument, plea, or countervailing proof. Abbott.\n\n1. To retire; to recoil. [Obs.] Spenser. 2. (Law)  To make, or put in, an answer, as to a plaintiff's surrejoinder. The plaintiff may answer the rejoinder by a surrejoinder; on which the defendant. Blackstone.
