sot
1. A stupid person; a blockhead; a dull fellow; a dolt. [Obs.] outh. In Egypt oft has seen the sot bow down, And reverence some dOldham. 2. A person stupefied by excessive drinking; an habitual drunkard. \"A brutal sot.\" Granville. Every sign That calls the staring sots to nasty wine. Roscommon.\n\nSottish; foolish; stupid; dull. [Obs.] \"Rich, but sot.\" Marston.\n\nTo stupefy; to infatuate; to besot. [R.] I hate to see a brave, bold fellow sotted. Dryden.\n\nTo tipple to stupidity. [R.] Goldsmith.
