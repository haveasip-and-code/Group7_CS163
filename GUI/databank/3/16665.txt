dowse
1. To plunge, or duck into water; to immerse; to douse. 2. Etym: [Cf. OD. doesen to strike, Norw. dusa to break.]  To beat or thrash. [Prov. Eng.] Halliwell.\n\nTo use the dipping or divining rod, as in search of water, ore, etc. Adams had the reputation of having dowsed successfully for more than a hundred wells. Eng. Cyc.\n\nA blow on the face. [Low] Colman.
