profligate
1. Overthrown; beaten; conquered. [Obs.] The foe is profligate, and run. Hudibras. 2. Broken down in respect of rectitude, principle, virtue, or decency; openly and shamelessly immoral or vicious; dissolute; as, profligate man or wretch. A race more profligate than we. Roscommon. Made prostitute and profligate muse. Dryden. Syn. -- Abandoned; corrupt; dissolute; vitiated; depraved; vicious; wicked. See Abandoned.\n\nAn abandoned person; one openly and shamelessly vicious; a dissolute person. \"Such a profligate as Antony.\" Swift.\n\nTo drive away; to overcome. Note: [A Latinism] [Obs.] Harvey.
