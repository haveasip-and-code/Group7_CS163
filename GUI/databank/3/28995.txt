clot
A concretion or coagulation; esp. a soft, slimy, coagulated mass, as of blood; a coagulum. \"Clots of pory gore.\" Addison. Doth bake the egg into clots as if it began to poach. Bacon. Note: Clod and clot appear to be radically the same word, and are so used by early writers; but in present use clod is applied to a mass of earth or the like, and clot to a concretion or coagulation of soft matter.\n\nTo concrete, coagulate, or thicken, as soft or fluid matter by evaporation; to become a cot or clod.\n\nTo form into a slimy mass.
