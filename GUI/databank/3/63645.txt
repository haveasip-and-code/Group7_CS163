snug
1. Close and warm; as, an infant lies snug. 2. Close; concealed; not exposed to notice. Lie snug, and hear what critics say. Swift. 3. Compact, convenient, and comfortable; as, a snug farm, house, or property.\n\nSame as Lug, n., 3.\n\nTo lie close; to snuggle; to snudge; -- often with up, or together; as, a child snugs up to its mother.\n\n1. To place snugly. [R.] Goldsmith. 2. To rub, as twine or rope, so as to make it smooth and improve the finish.
