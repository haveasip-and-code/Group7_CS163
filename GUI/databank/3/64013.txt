tollbooth
1. A place where goods are weighed to ascertain the duties or toll. [Obs.] He saw Levy . . . sitting at the tollbooth. Wyclif (Mark ii. 14). 2. In Scotland, a burgh jail; hence, any prison, especially a town jail. Sir W. Scott.\n\nTo imprison in a tollbooth. [R.] That they might tollbooth Oxford men. Bp. Corbet.
