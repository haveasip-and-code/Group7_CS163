bother
To annoy; to trouble; to worry; to perplex. See Pother. Note: The imperative is sometimes used as an exclamation mildly imprecatory.\n\nTo feel care or anxiety; to make or take trouble; to be troublesome. Without bothering about it. H. James.\n\nOne who, or that which, bothers; state of perplexity or annoyance; embarrassment; worry; disturbance; petty trouble; as, to be in a bother.
