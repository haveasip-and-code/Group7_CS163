barrack
1. (Mil.)  A building for soldiers, especially when in garrison. Commonly in the pl., originally meaning temporary huts, but now usually applied to a permanent structure or set of buildings. He lodged in a miserable hut or barrack, composed of dry branches and thatched with straw. Gibbon. 2. A movable roof sliding on four posts, to cover hay, straw, etc. [Local, U.S.]\n\nTo supply with barracks; to establish in barracks; as, to barrack troops.\n\nTo live or lodge in barracks.
