ho
Who. [Obs.] In some Chaucer MSS.\n\nA stop; a halt; a moderation of pace. There is no ho with them. Decker.\n\n1. Halloo! attend! -- a call to excite attention, or to give notice of approach. \"What noise there, ho\" Shak. \"Ho! who's within\" Shak. 2. Etym: [Perhaps corrupted fr. hold; but cf. F. hau stop! and E. whoa.]  Stop! stand still! hold! -- a word now used by teamsters, but formerly to order the cessation of anything. [Written also whoa, and, formerly, hoo.] The duke . . . pulled out his sword and cried \"Hoo!\" Chaucer. An herald on a scaffold made an hoo. Chaucer.
