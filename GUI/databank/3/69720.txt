strand
One of the twists, or strings, as of fibers, wires, etc., of which a rope is composed.\n\nTo break a strand of (a rope).\n\nThe shore, especially the beach of a sea, ocean, or large lake; rarely, the margin of a navigable river. Chaucer. Strand birds. (Zoöl.) See Shore birds, under Shore. -- Strand plover (Zoöl.), a black-bellied plover. See Illust. of Plover. -- Strand wolf (Zoöl.), the brown hyena.\n\nTo drive on a strand; hence, to run aground; as, to strand a ship.\n\nTo drift, or be driven, on shore to run aground; as, the ship stranded at high water.
