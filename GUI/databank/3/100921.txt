twire
A twisted filament; a thread. [Obs.] Locke.\n\n1. To peep; to glance obliquely; to leer. [Obs.] Which maids will twire 'tween their fingers. B. Jonson. I saw the wench that twired and twinkled at thee. Beau. & Fl. 2. To twinkle; to glance; to gleam. [Obs.] When sparkling stars twire not. Shak.\n\nTo sing, or twitter. [Obs.] Chaucer.
