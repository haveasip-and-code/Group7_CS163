metathesis
1. (Gram.)  Transposition, as of the letters or syllables of a word; as, pistris for pristis; meagre for meager. 2. (Med.)  A mere change in place of a morbid substance, without removal from the body. 3. (Chem.)  The act, process, or result of exchange, substitution, or replacement of atoms and radicals; thus, by metathesis an acid gives up all or part of its hydrogen, takes on an equivalent amount of a metal or base, and forms a salt.
