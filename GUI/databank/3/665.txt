surplusage
1. Surplus; excess; overplus; as, surplusage of grain or goods beyond what is wanted. Take what thou please of all this surplusage. Spenser. A surplusage given to one part is paid out of a reduction from another part of the same creature. Emerson. 2. (Law)  Matter in pleading which is not necessary or relevant to the case, and which may be rejected. 3. (Accounts)  A greater disbursement than the charge of the accountant amounts to. [Obs.] Rees.
