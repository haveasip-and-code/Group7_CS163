honorary
1. A fee offered to professional men for their services; as, an honorarium of one thousand dollars. S. Longfellow. 2. (Law)  An honorary payment, usually in recognition of services for which it is not usual or not lawful to assign a fixed business price. Heumann.\n\n1. Done as a sign or evidence of honor; as, honorary services. Macaulay. 2. Conferring honor, or intended merely to confer honor without emolument; as, an honorary degree. \"Honorary arches.\" Addison. 3. Holding a title or place without rendering service or receiving reward; as, an honorary member of a society.
