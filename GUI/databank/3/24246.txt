knoll
A little round hill; a mound; a small elevation of earth; the top or crown of a hill. On knoll or hillock rears his crest, Lonely and huge, the giant oak. Sir W. Scott.\n\nTo ring, as a bell; to strike a knell upon; to toll; to proclaim, or summon, by ringing. \"Knolled to church.\" Shak. Heavy clocks knolling the drowsy hours. Tennyson.\n\nTo sound, as a bell; to knell. Shak. For a departed being's soul The death hymn peals, and the hollow bells knoll. Byron.\n\nThe tolling of a bell; a knell. [R.] Byron.
