limitary
1. Placed at the limit, as a guard. \"Proud limitary cherub.\" Milton. 2. Confined within limits; limited in extent, authority, power, etc. \"The limitary ocean.\" Trench. The poor, limitary creature calling himself a man of the world. De Quincey. 3. Limiting, or tending to limit; restrictive. Doctrines limitary, if not subversive of the papal power. Milman.\n\n1. That which serves to limit; a boundary; border land. [Obs.] Fuller. 2. A limiter. See Limiter, 2.
