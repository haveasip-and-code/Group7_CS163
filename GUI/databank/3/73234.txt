wend
p. p. of Wene. Chaucer.\n\n1. To go; to pass; to betake one's self. \"To Canterbury they wend.\" Chaucer. To Athens shall the lovers wend. Shak. 2. To turn round. [Obs.] Sir W. Raleigh.\n\nTo direct; to betake;- used chiefly in the phrase to wend one's way. Also used reflexively. \"Great voyages to wend.\" Surrey.\n\nA large extent of ground; a perambulation; a circuit. [Obs.] Burrill.
