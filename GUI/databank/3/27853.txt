auspicious
1. Having omens or tokens of a favorable issue; giving promise of success, prosperity, or happiness; predicting good; as, an auspicious beginning. Auspicious union of order and freedom. Macaulay. 2. Prosperous; fortunate; as, auspicious years. \"Auspicious chief.\" Dryden. 3. Favoring; favorable; propitious; -- applied to persons or things. \"Thy auspicious mistress.\" Shak. \"Auspicious gales.\" Pope. Syn. -- See Propitious. -- Aus*pi\"cious*ly, adv. -- Aus*pi\"cious*ness, n.
