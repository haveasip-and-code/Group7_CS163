rime
A rent or long aperture; a chink; a fissure; a crack. Sir T. Browne.\n\nWhite frost; hoarfrost; congealed dew or vapor. The trees were now covered with rime. De Quincey.\n\nTo freeze or congeal into hoarfrost.\n\nA step or round of a ladder; a rung.\n\nRhyme. See Rhyme. Coleridge. Landor. Note: This spelling, which is etymologically preferable, is coming into use again.\n\nTo rhyme. See Rhyme.
