sough
A sow. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\nA small drain; an adit. [Prov. Eng.] W. M. Buchanan.\n\n1. The sound produced by soughing; a hollow murmur or roaring. The whispering leaves or solemn sough of the forest. W. Howitt. 2. Hence, a vague rumor or flying report. [Scot.] 3. A cant or whining mode of speaking, especially in preaching or praying. [Scot.] Jamieson.\n\nTo whistle or sigh, as the wind.
