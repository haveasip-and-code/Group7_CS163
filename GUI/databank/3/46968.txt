dint
1. A blow; a stroke. [Obs.] \"Mortal dint.\" Milton. \"Like thunder's dint.\" Fairfax. 2. The mark left by a blow; an indentation or impression made by violence; a dent. Dryden. Every dint a sword had beaten in it [the shield]. Tennyson. 3. Force; power; -- esp. in the phrase by dint of. Now you weep; and, I perceive, you feel The dint of pity. Shak. It was by dint of passing strength That he moved the massy stone at length. Sir W. Scott.\n\nTo make a mark or cavity on or in, by a blow or by pressure; to dent. Donne. Tennyson.
