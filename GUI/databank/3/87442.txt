replevy
1. (Law)  To take or get back, by a writ for that purpose (goods and chattels wrongfuly taken or detained), upon giving security to try the right to them in a suit at law, and, if that should be determined against the plaintiff, to return the property replevied. 2. (Old Eng.LAw)  To bail. Spenser.\n\nReplevin. Mozley & W.
