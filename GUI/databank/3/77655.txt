thee
To thrive; to prosper. [Obs.] \"He shall never thee.\" Chaucer. Well mote thee, as well can wish your thought. Spenser.\n\nThe objective case of thou. See Thou. Note: Thee is poetically used for thyself, as him for himself, etc. This sword hath ended him; so shall it thee, Unless thou yield thee as my prisoner. Shak.
