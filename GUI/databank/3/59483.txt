travail
1. Labor with pain; severe toil or exertion. As everything of price, so this doth require travail. Hooker. 2. Parturition; labor; as, an easy travail.\n\n1. To labor with pain; to toil. [Archaic] \"Slothful persons which will not travail for their livings.\" Latimer. 2. To suffer the pangs of childbirth; to be in labor.\n\nTo harass; to tire. [Obs.] As if all these troubles had not been sufficient to travail the realm, a great division fell among the nobility. Hayward.
