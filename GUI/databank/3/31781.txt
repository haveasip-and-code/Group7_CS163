slime
1. Soft, moist earth or clay, having an adhesive quality; viscous mud. As it [Nilus] ebbs, the seedsman Upon the slime and ooze scatters his grain. Shak. 2. Any mucilaginous substance; any substance of a dirty nature, that is moist, soft, and adhesive. 3. (Script.)  Bitumen. [Archaic] Slime had they for mortar. Gen. xi. 3. 4. pl. (Mining)  Mud containing metallic ore, obtained in the preparatory dressing. Pryce. 5. (Physiol.)  A mucuslike substance which exudes from the bodies of certain animals. Goldsmith. Slime eel.
