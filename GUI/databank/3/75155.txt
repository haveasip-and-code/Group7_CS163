weal
The mark of a stripe. See Wale.\n\nTo mark with stripes. See Wale.\n\n1. A sound, healthy, or prosperous state of a person or thing; prosperity; happiness; welfare. God . . . grant you wele and prosperity. Chaucer. As we love the weal of our souls and bodies. Bacon. To him linked in weal or woe. Milton. Never was there a time when it more concerned the public weal that the character of the Parliament should stand high. Macaulay. 2. The body politic; the state; common wealth. [Obs.] The special watchmen of our English weal. Shak.\n\nTo promote the weal of; to cause to be prosperous. [Obs.] Beau. & Fl.
