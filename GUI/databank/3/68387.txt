bowery
Shading, like a bower; full of bowers. A bowery maze that shades the purple streams. Trumbull.\n\nA farm or plantation with its buildings. [U.S.Hist.] The emigrants [in New York] were scattered on boweries or plantations; and seeing the evils of this mode of living widely apart, they were advised, in 1643 and 1646, by the Dutch authorities, to gather into \"villages, towns, and hamlets, as the English were in the habit of doing.\" Bancroft.\n\nCharacteristic of the street called the Bowery, in New York city; swaggering; flashy.
