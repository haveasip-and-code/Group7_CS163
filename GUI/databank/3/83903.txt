delate
1. To carry; to convey. Try exactly the time wherein sound is delated. Bacon. 2. To carry abroad; to spread; to make public. When the crime is delated or notorious. Jer. Taylor. 3. To carry or bring against, as a charge; to inform against; to accuse; to denounce. As men were delated, they were marked down for such a fine. Bp. Burnet. 4. To carry on; to conduct. Warner.\n\nTo dilate. [Obs.] Goodwin.
