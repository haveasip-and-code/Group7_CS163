foible
Weak; feeble. [Obs.] Lord Herbert.\n\n1. A moral weakness; a failing; a weak point; a frailty. A disposition radically noble and generous, clouded and overshadowed by superficial foibles. De Quincey. 2. The half of a sword blade or foil blade nearest the point; -- opposed to forte. [Written also faible.] Syn. -- Fault; imperfection; failing; weakness; infirmity; frailty; defect. See Fault.
