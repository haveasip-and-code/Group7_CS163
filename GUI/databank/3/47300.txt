revile
To address or abuse with opprobrious and contemptuous language; to reproach. \"And did not she herself revile me there\" Shak. Who, when he was reviled, reviled not again. 1 Pet. ii. 23. Syn. -- To reproach; vilify; upbraid; calumniate.\n\nReproach; reviling. [Obs.] The gracious Judge, without revile, replied. Milton.
