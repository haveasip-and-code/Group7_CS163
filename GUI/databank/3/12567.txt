astrict
1. To bind up; to confine; to constrict; to contract. The solid parts were to be relaxed or astricted. Arbuthnot. 2. To bind; to constrain; to restrict; to limit. [R.] The mind is astricted to certain necessary modes or forms of thought. Sir W. Hamilton. 3. (Scots Law)  To restrict the tenure of; as, to astrict lands. See Astriction, 4. Burrill.\n\nConcise; contracted. [Obs.] Weever.
