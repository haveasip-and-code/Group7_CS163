annex
1. To join or attach; usually to subjoin; to affix; to append; -- followed by to. \"He annexed a codicil to a will.\" Johnson. 2. To join or add, as a smaller thing to a greater. He annexed a province to his kingdom. Johnson. 3. To attach or connect, as a consequence, condition, etc.; as, to annex a penalty to a prohibition, or punishment to guilt. Syn. -- To add; append; affix; unite; coalesce. See Add.\n\nTo join; to be united. Tooke.\n\nSomething annexed or appended; as, an additional stipulation to a writing, a subsidiary building to a main building; a wing.
