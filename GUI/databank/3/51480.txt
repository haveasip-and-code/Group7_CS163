sew
Juice; gravy; a seasoned dish; a delicacy. [Obs.] Gower. I will not tell of their strange sewes. Chaucer.\n\nTo follow; to pursue; to sue. [Obs.] Chaucer. Spenser.\n\n1. To unite or fasten together by stitches, as with a needle and thread. No man also seweth a piece of new cloth on an old garment. Mark ii. 21. 2. To close or stop by ssewing; -- often with up; as, to sew up a rip. 3. To inclose by sewing; -- sometimes with up; as, to sew money in a bag.\n\nTo practice sewing; to work with needle and thread.\n\nTo drain, as a pond, for taking the fish. [Obs.] Tusser.
