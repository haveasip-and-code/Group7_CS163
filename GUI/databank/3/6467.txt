conceited
1. Endowed with fancy or imagination. [Obs.] He was . . . pleasantly conceited, and sharp of wit. Knolles. 2. Entertaining a flattering opinion of one's self; vain. If you think me too conceited Or to passion quickly heated. Swift. Conceited of their own wit, science, and politeness. Bentley. 3. Curiously contrived or designed; fanciful. [Obs.] A conceited chair to sleep in. Evelyn. Syn. -- Vain; proud; opinionated; egotistical.
