decussate
To cross at an acute angle; to cut or divide in the form of X; to intersect; -- said of lines in geometrical figures, rays of light, nerves, etc.\n\n1. Crossed; intersected. 2. (Bot.)  Growing in pairs, each of which is at right angles to the next pair above or below; as, decussated leaves or branches. 3. (Rhet.)  Consisting of two rising and two falling clauses, placed in alternate opposition to each other; as, a decussated period.
