garb
1. (a) Clothing in general. (b) The whole dress or suit of clothes worn by any person, especially when indicating rank or office; as, the garb of a clergyman or a judge. (c) Costume; fashion; as, the garb of a gentleman in the 16th century. 2. External appearance, as expressive of the feelings or character; looks; fashion or manner, as of speech. You thought, because he could not speak English in the native garb, he could not therefore handle an English cudgel. Shak.\n\nA sheaf of grain (wheat, unless otherwise specified).\n\nTo clothe; array; deck. These black dog-Dons Garb themselves bravely. Tennyson.
