concordance
1. Agreement; accordance. Contrasts, and yet concordances. Carlyle. 2. (Gram.)  Concord; agreement. [Obs.] Aschlam. 3. An alphabetical verbal index showing the places in the text of a book where each principal word may be found, with its immediate context in each place. His knowledge of the Bible was such, that he might have been called a living concordance. Macaulay. 4. A topical index or orderly analysis of the contents of a book.
