dicker
1. The number or quantity of ten, particularly ten hides or skins; a dakir; as, a dicker of gloves. [Obs.] A dicker of cowhides. Heywood. 2. A chaffering, barter, or exchange, of small wares; as, to make a dicker. [U.S.] For peddling dicker, not for honest sales. Whittier.\n\nTo negotiate a dicker; to barter. [U.S.] \"Ready to dicker. and to swap.\" Cooper.
