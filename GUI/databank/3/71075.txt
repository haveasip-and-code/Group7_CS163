-ing
1. Etym: [For OE. -and, -end, -ind, AS. -ende; akin to Goth. -and-, L. -ant-, -ent-, Gr.  A suffix used to from present participles; as, singing, playing. 2. Etym: [OE. -ing, AS. -ing, -ung.]  A suffix used to form nouns from verbs, and signifying the act of; the result of the act; as, riding, dying, feeling. It has also a secondary collective force; as, shipping, clothing. Note: The Old English ending of the present participle and verbal noun became confused, both becoming -ing. 3. Etym: [AS. -ing.]  A suffix formerly used to form diminutives; as, lording, farthing.
