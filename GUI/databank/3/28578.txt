corrigible
1. Capable of being set right, amended, or reformed; as, a corrigible fault. 2. Submissive to correction; docile. \"Bending down his corrigible neck.\" Shak. 3. Deserving chastisement; punishable. [Obs.] He was taken up very short, and adjudged corrigible for such presumptuous language. Howell. 4. Having power to correct; corrective. [Obs.] The . . . .corrigible authority of this lies in our wills. Shak.
