holt
3d pers. sing. pres. of Hold, contr. from holdeth. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\n1. A piece of woodland; especially, a woody hill. \"Every holt and heath.\" Chaucer. She sent her voice though all the holt Before her, and the park. Tennyson. 2. A deep hole in a river where there is protection for fish; also, a cover, a hole, or hiding place. \" The fox has gone to holt.\" C. Kingsley.
