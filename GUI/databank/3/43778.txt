portcullis
1. (Fort.)  A grating of iron or of timbers pointed with iron, hung over the gateway of a fortress, to be let down to prevent the entrance of an enemy. \"Let the portcullis fall.\" Sir W. Scott. She . . . the huge portcullis high updrew. Milton. 2. An English coin of the reign of Elizabeth, struck for the use of the East India Company; -- so called from its bearing the figure of a portcullis on the reverse.\n\nTo obstruct with, or as with, a portcullis; to shut; to bar. [R.] Shak.
