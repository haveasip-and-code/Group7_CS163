extempore
Without previous study or meditation; without preparation; on the spur of the moment; suddenly; extemporaneously; as, to write or speak extempore. Shak. -- a.  Done or performed extempore. \"Extempore dissertation.\" Addison. \"Extempore poetry.\" Dryden. -- n.  Speaking or writing done extempore. [Obs.] Bp. Fell.
