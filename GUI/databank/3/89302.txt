trist
To trust. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\n1. Trust. [Obs.] 2. A post, or station, in hunting. [Obs.] Chaucer. 3. A secret meeting, or the place of such meeting; a tryst. See Tryst. [Obs.] George Douglas caused a trist to be set between him and the cardinal and four lords; at the which trist he and the cardinal agreed finally. Letter dated Sept., 1543.\n\nSad; sorrowful; gloomy. [Obs.] Fairfax.
