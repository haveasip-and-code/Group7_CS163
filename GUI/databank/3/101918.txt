garble
1. To sift or bolt, to separate the fine or valuable parts of from the coarse and useless parts, or from dros or dirt; as, to garble spices. [Obs.] 2. To pick out such parts of as may serve a purpose; to mutilate; to pervert; as, to garble a quotation; to garble an account.\n\n1. Refuse; rubbish. [Obs.] Wolcott. 2. pl.  Impurities separated from spices, drugs, etc.; -- also called garblings.
