immerge
To plungel into, under, or within anything especially a fuid; to dip; to immerse. See Immerse. We took . . . lukewarm water, and in it immerged a quantity of the leaves of senna. Boyle. Their souls are immerged in matter. Jer. Taylor.\n\nTo dissapear by entering into any medium, as a star into the light of the sun. [R.]
