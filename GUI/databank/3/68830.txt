crock
The loose black particles collected from combustion, as on pots and kettles, or in a chimney; soot; smut; also, coloring matter which rubs off from cloth.\n\nTo soil by contact, as with soot, or with the coloring matter of badly dyed cloth.\n\nTo give off crock or smut.\n\nA low stool. \"I . . . seated her upon a little crock.\" Tatler.\n\nAny piece of crockery, especially of coarse earthenware; an earthen pot or pitcher. Like foolish flies about an honey crock. Spenser.\n\nTo lay up in a crock; as, to crock butter. Halliwell.
