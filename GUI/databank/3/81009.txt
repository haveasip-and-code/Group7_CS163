tucker
1. One who, or that which, tucks; specifically, an instrument with which tuck are made. 2. A narrow piece of linen or the like, folded across the breast, or attached to the gown at the neck, forming a part of a woman's dress in the 17th century and later. 3. Etym: [See Tuck, v. t., 4.]  A fuller. [Prov. Eng.]\n\nTo tire; to weary; -- usually with out. [Colloq. U. S.]
