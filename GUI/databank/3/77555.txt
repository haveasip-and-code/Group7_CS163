cease
1. To come to an end; to stop; to leave off or give over; to desist; as, the noise ceased \"To cease from strife.\" Prov. xx. 3. 2. To be wanting; to fail; to pass away. The poor shall never cease out of the land. Deut. xv. 11. Syn. -- To intermit; desist; stop; abstain; quit; discontinue; refrain; leave off; pause; end.\n\nTo put a stop to; to bring to an end. But he, her fears to cease Sent down the meek-eyed peace. Milton. Cease, then, this impious rage. Milton\n\nExtinction. [Obs.] Shak.
