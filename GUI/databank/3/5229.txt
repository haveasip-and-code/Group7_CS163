jactitation
1. (Law)  Vain boasting or assertions repeated to the prejudice of another's right; false claim. Mozley & W. 2. (Med.)  A frequent tossing or moving of the body; restlessness, as in delirium. Dunglison. Jactitation of marriage (Eng. Eccl. Law), a giving out or boasting by a party that he or she is married to another, whereby a common reputation of their matrimony may ensue. Blackstone.
