big
1. Having largeness of size; of much bulk or magnitude; of great size; large. \"He's too big to go in there.\" Shak. 2. Great with young; pregnant; swelling; ready to give birth or produce; -- often figuratively. [Day] big with the fate of Cato and of Rome. Addison. 3. Having greatness, fullness, importance, inflation, distention, etc., whether in a good or a bad sense; as, a big heart; a big voice; big looks; to look big. As applied to looks, it indicates haughtiness or pride. God hath not in heaven a bigger argument. Jer. Taylor. Note: Big is often used in self-explaining compounds; as, big-boned; big-sounding; big-named; big-voiced. To talk big, to talk loudly, arrogantly, or pretentiously. I talked big to them at first. De Foe. Syn. -- Bulky; large; great; massive; gross.\n\nBarley, especially the hardy four-rowed kind. \"Bear interchanges in local use, now with barley, now with bigg.\" New English Dict.\n\nTo build. [Scot. & North of Eng. Dial.] Sir W. Scott.
