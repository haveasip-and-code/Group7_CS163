kern
1. A light-armed foot soldier of the ancient militia of Ireland and Scotland; -- distinguished from gallowglass, and often used as a term of contempt. Macaulay. Now for our Irish wars; We must supplant those rough, rug-headed kerns. Shak. 2. Any kind of boor or low-lived person. [Obs.] Blount. 3. (O. Eng. Law)  An idler; a vagabond. Wharton.\n\nA part of the face of a type which projects beyond the body, or shank.\n\nTo form with a kern. See 2d Kern.\n\nA churn. [Prov. Eng.]\n\nA hand mill. See Quern. Johnson.\n\n1. To harden, as corn in ripening. [Obs.] Carew. 2. To take the form of kernels; to granulate. [Obs.] It is observed that rain makes the salt kern. Dampier.
