slant
To be turned or inclined from a right line or level; to lie obliquely; to slope. On the side of younder slanting hill. Dodsley.\n\nTo turn from a direct line; to give an oblique or sloping direction to; as, to slant a line.\n\n1. A slanting direction or plane; a slope; as, it lies on a slant. 2. An oblique reflection or gibe; a sarcastic remark. Slant or wind, a local variation of the wind from its general direction.\n\nInclined from a direct line, whether horizontal or perpendicular; sloping; oblique. \"The slant lightning.\" Milton.
