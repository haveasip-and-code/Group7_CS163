chaplet
1. A garland or wreath to be worn on the head. 2. A string of beads, or part of a string, used by Roman Catholic in praying; a third of a rosary, or fifty beads. Her chaplet of beads and her missal. Longfellow. 3. (Arch.)  A small molding, carved into beads, pearls, olives, etc. 4. (Man.)  A chapelet. See Chapelet, 1. 5. (Founding)  A bent piece of sheet iron, or a pin with thin plates on its ends, for holding a core in place in the mold. 6. A tuft of feathers on a peacock's head. Johnson.\n\nA small chapel or shrine.\n\nTo adorn with a chaplet or with flowers. R. Browning.
