disembogue
1. To pour out or discharge at the mouth, as a stream; to vent; to discharge into an ocean, a lake, etc. Rolling down, the steep Timavdisembogues his waves. Addison. 2. To eject; to cast forth. [R.] Swift.\n\nTo become discharged; to flow put; to find vent; to pour out contents. Volcanos bellow ere they disembogue. Young.
