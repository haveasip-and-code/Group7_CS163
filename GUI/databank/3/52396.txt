parole
1. A word; an oral utterance. [Obs.] 2. Word of promise; word of honor; plighted faith; especially (Mil.), promise, upon one's faith and honor, to fulfill stated conditions, as not to bear arms against one's captors, to return to custody, or the like. This man had forfeited his military parole. Macaulay. 3. (Mil.)  A watchword given only to officers of guards; -- distinguished from countersign, which is given to all guards. 4. (Law)  Oral declaration. See lst Parol, 2.\n\nSee 2d Parol.\n\nTo set at liberty on parole; as, to parole prisoners.
