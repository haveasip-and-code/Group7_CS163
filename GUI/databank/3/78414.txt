slue
A slough; a run or wet place. See 2d Slough, 2.\n\n1. (Naut.)  To turn about a fixed point, usually the center or axis, as a spar or piece of timber; to turn; -- used also of any heavy body. 2. In general, to turn about; to twist; -- often used reflexively and followed by round. [Colloq.] They laughed, and slued themselves round. Dickens.\n\nTo turn about; to turn from the course; to slip or slide and turn from an expected or desired course; -- often followed by round.\n\nSee Sloough, 2. [Local]
