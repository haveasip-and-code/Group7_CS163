quarry
Same as 1st Quarrel. [Obs.] Fairfax.\n\nQuadrate; square. [Obs.]\n\n1. (a) A part of the entrails of the beast taken, given to the hounds. (b) A heap of game killed. 2. The object of the chase; the animal hunted for; game; especially, the game hunted with hawks. \"The stone-dead quarry.\" Spenser. The wily quarry shunned the shock. Sir W. Scott.\n\nTo secure prey; to prey, as a vulture or harpy. L'Estrange.\n\nA place, cavern, or pit where stone is taken from the rock or ledge, or dug from the earth, for building or other purposes; a stone pit. See 5th Mine (a).\n\nTo dig or take from a quarry; as, to quarry marble.
