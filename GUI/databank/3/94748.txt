plash
1. A small pool of standing water; a puddle. Bacon. \"These shallow plashes.\" Barrow. 2. A dash of water; a splash.\n\nTo dabble in water; to splash. \"Plashing among bedded pebbles.\" Keats. Far below him plashed the waters. Longfellow.\n\n1. To splash, as water. 2. To splash or sprinkle with coloring matter; as, to plash a wall in imitation of granite.\n\nTo cut partly, or to bend and intertwine the branches of; as, to plash a hedge. Evelyn.\n\nThe branch of a tree partly cut or bent, and bound to, or intertwined with, other branches.
