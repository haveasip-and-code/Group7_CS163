daw
A European bird of the Crow family (Corvus monedula), often nesting in church towers and ruins; a jackdaw. The loud daw, his throat displaying, draw The whole assembly of his fellow daws. Waller. Note: The daw was reckoned as a silly bird, and a daw meant a simpleton. See in Shakespeare: -- \"Then thou dwellest with daws too.\" (Coriolanus iv. 5, 1. 47.) Skeat.\n\nTo dawn. [Obs.] See Dawn.\n\n1. To rouse. [Obs.] 2. To daunt; to terrify. [Obs.] B. Jonson.
