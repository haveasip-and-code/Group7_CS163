encamp
To form and occupy a camp; to prepare and settle in temporary habitations, as tents or huts; to halt on a march, pitch tents, or form huts, and remain for the night or for a longer time, as an army or a company traveling. The host of the Philistines encamped in the valley of Rephaim. 1 Chron. xi. 15.\n\nTo form into a camp; to place in a temporary habitation, or quarters. Bid him encamp his soldiers. Shak.
