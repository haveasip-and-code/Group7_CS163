aggregate
1. Formed by a collection of particulars into a whole mass or sum; collective. The aggregate testimony of many hundreds. Sir T. Browne. 2.  (Anat.) Formed into clusters or groups of lobules; as, aggregate glands. 3.  (Bot.) Composed of several florets within a common involucre, as in the daisy; or of several carpels formed from one flower, as in the raspberry. 4.  (Min. & Geol.) Having the several component parts adherent to each other only to such a degree as to be separable by mechanical means. 5.  (Zo√∂l.)
