potent
1. Producing great physical effects; forcible; powerful' efficacious; as, a potent medicine. \"Harsh and potent injuries.\" Shak. Moses once more his potent rod extends. Milton. 2. Having great authority, control, or dominion; puissant; mighty; influential; as, a potent prince. \"A potent dukedom.\" Shak. Most potent, grave, and reverend signiors. Shak. 3. Powerful, in an intellectual or moral sense; having great influence; as, potent interest; a potent argument. Cross potent. (Her.) See Illust. (7) of Cross. Syn. -- Powerful; mighty; puissant; strong; able; efficient; forcible; efficacious; cogent; influential.\n\n1. A prince; a potentate. [Obs.] Shak. 2. Etym: [See Potence.]  A staff or crutch. [Obs.] 3. (Her.)  One of the furs; a surface composed of patches which are supposed to represent crutch heads; they are always alternately argent and azure, unless otherwise specially mentioned. Counter potent (Her.), a fur differing from potent in the arrangement of the patches.
