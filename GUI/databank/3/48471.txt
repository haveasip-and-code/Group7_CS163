See Leatherwood. WIDAL'S TEST; WIDAL TEST; WIDAL'S REACTION; WIDAL REACTION Wi*dal's\", or Wi*dal\", test or reaction . [After Fernand Widal (b. 1862), French physician.] (Med.)  A test for typhoid fever based on the fact that blood serum of one affected, in a bouillon culture of typhoid bacilli, causes the bacilli to agglutinate and lose their motility.
