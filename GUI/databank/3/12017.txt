transfuse
1. To pour, as liquid, out of one vessel into another; to transfer by pouring. 2 2 (Med.)  To transfer, as blood, from the veins or arteries of one man or animal to those of another. 3. To cause to pass from to another; to cause to be instilled or imbibed; as, to transfuse a spirit of patriotism into a man; to transfuse a love of letters. Into thee such virtue and grace Immense I have transfused. Milton.
