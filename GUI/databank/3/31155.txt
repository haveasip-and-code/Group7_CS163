flout
To mock or insult; to treat with contempt. Phillida flouts me. Walton. Three gaudy standarts lout the pale blue sky. Byron.\n\nTo practice mocking; to behave with contempt; to sneer; to fleer; -- often with at. Fleer and gibe, and laugh and flout. Swift.\n\nA mock; an insult. Who put your beauty to this flout and scorn. Tennyson.
