niggard
A person meanly close and covetous; one who spends grudgingly; a stingy, parsimonous fellow; a miser. Chaucer. A penurious niggard of his wealth. Milton. Be niggards of advice on no pretense. Pope.\n\nLike a niggard; meanly covetous or parsimonious; niggardly; miserly; stingy.\n\nTo act the niggard toward; to be niggardly. [R.] Shak.
