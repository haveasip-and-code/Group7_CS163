obligate
1. To bring or place under obligation, moral or legal; to hold by a constraining motive. \"Obligated by a sense of duty.\" Proudfit. That's your true plan -- to obligate The present ministers of state. Churchill. 2. To bind or firmly hold to an act; to compel; to constrain; to bind to any act of duty or courtesy by a formal pledge. That they may not incline or be obligated to any vile or lowly occupations. Landor.
