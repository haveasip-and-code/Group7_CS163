quip
A smart, sarcastic turn or jest; a taunt; a severe retort; a gibe. Quips, and cranks, and wanton wiles. Milton. He was full of joke and jest, But all his merry quips are o'er. Tennyson.\n\nTo taunt; to treat with quips. The more he laughs, and does her closely quip. Spenser.\n\nTo scoff; to use taunts. Sir H. Sidney.
