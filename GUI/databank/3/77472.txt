blare
To sound loudly and somewhat harshly. \"The trumpet blared.\" Tennyson.\n\nTo cause to sound like the blare of a trumpet; to proclaim loudly. To blare its own interpretation. Tennyson.\n\nThe harsh noise of a trumpet; a loud and somewhat harsh noise, like the blast of a trumpet; a roar or bellowing. With blare of bugle, clamor of men. Tennyson. His ears are stunned with the thunder's blare. J. R. Drake.
