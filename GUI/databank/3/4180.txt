flaunt
To throw or spread out; to flutter; to move ostentatiously; as, a flaunting show. You flaunt about the streets in your new gilt chariot. Arbuthnot. One flaunts in rags, one flutters in brocade. Pope.\n\nTo display ostentatiously; to make an impudent show of.\n\nAnything displayed for show. [Obs.] In these my borrowed flaunts. Shak.
