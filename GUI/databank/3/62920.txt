smug
Studiously neat or nice, especially in dress; spruce; affectedly precise; smooth and prim. They be so smug and smooth. Robynson (More's Utopia). The smug and scanty draperies of his style. De Quincey. A young, smug, handsome holiness has no fellow. Beau & Fl.\n\nTo make smug, or spruce. [Obs.] Thus said, he smugged his beard, and stroked up fair. Dryton.
