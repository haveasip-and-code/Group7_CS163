valiant
1. Vigorous in body; strong; powerful; as, a valiant fencer. [Obs.] Walton. 2. Intrepid in danger; courageous; brave. A valiant and most expert gentleman. Shak. And Saul said to David . . . be thou valiant for me, and fight the Lord's battles. 1 Sam. xviii. 17. 3. Performed with valor or bravery; heroic. \"Thou bearest the highest name for valiant acts.\" Milton. [The saints] have made such valiant confessions. J. H. Newman. -- Val\"iant*ly, adv. -- Val\"iant*ness, n.
