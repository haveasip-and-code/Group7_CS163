wimble
An instrument for boring holes, turned by a handle. Specifically: (a) A gimlet. \" It is but like the little wimble, to let in the greater auger.\" Selden. (b) A stonecutter's brace for boring holes in stone. (c) An auger used for boring in earth.\n\nTo bore or pierce, as with a wimble. \"A foot soldier . . . wimbled also a hole through said coffin.\" Wood.\n\nActive; nimble.[Obs.] Spenser.
