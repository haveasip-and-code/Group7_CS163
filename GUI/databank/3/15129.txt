prow
The fore part of a vessel; the bow; the stem; hence, the vessel itself. Wordsworth. The floating vessel swum Uplifted, and secure with beaked prow rode tilting o'er the waves. Milton.\n\nSee Proa.\n\nValiant; brave; gallant; courageous. [Archaic] Tennyson. The prowest knight that ever field did fight. Spenser.\n\nBenefit; profit; good; advantage. [Obs.] That shall be for your hele and for your prow. Chaucer.
