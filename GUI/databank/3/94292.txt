spat
imp. of Spit. [Obs. or R.]\n\nA young oyster or other bivalve mollusk, both before and after it first becomes adherent, or such young, collectively.\n\nTo emit spawn; to emit, as spawn.\n\n1. A light blow with something flat. [U.S. & Prov. Eng.] 2. Hence, a petty combat, esp. a verbal one; a little quarrel, dispute, or dissension. [U. S.]\n\nTo dispute. [R.] Smart.\n\nTo slap, as with the open hand; to clap together; as the hands. [Local, U.S.] Little Isabel leaped up and down, spatting her hands. Judd.
