buttress
1. (Arch.)  A projecting mass of masonry, used for resisting the thrust of an arch, or for ornament and symmetry. Note: When an external projection is used merely to stiffen a wall, it is a pier. 2. Anything which supports or strengthens. \"The ground pillar and buttress of the good old cause of nonconformity.\" South. Flying buttress. See Flying buttress.\n\nTo support with a buttress; to prop; to brace firmly. To set it upright again, and to prop and buttress it up for duration. Burke.
