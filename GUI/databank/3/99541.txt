reflective
1. Throwing back images; as, a reflective mirror. In the reflective stream the sighing bride, viewing her charms. Prior. 2. Capable of exercising thought or judgment; as, reflective reason. Prior. His perceptive and reflective faculties . . . thus acquired a precocious and extraordinary development. Motley. 3. Addicted to introspective or meditative habits; as, a reflective person. 4. (Gram.)  Reflexive; reciprocal. -- Re*flect\"ive*ly, adv. -- Re*flect\"ive*ness, n. \"Reflectiveness of manner.\" J. C. Shairp.
