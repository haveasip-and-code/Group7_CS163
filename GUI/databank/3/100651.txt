beef
1. An animal of the genus Bos, especially the common species, B. taurus, including the bull, cow, and ox, in their full grown state; esp., an ox or cow fattened for food. Note: [In this, which is the original sense, the word has a plural, beeves (.] A herd of beeves, fair oxen and fair kine. Milton. 2. The flesh of an ox, or cow, or of any adult bovine animal, when slaughtered for food. Note: [In this sense, the word has no plural.] \"Great meals of beef.\" Shak. 3. Applied colloquially to human flesh.\n\nOf, pertaining to, or resembling, beef. Beef tea, essence of beef, or strong beef broth.
