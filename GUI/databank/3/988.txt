stound
To be in pain or sorrow. [Obs. or Prov. Eng. & Scot.]\n\nStunned. [Obs.]\n\n1. A sudden, severe pain or grief; peril; alarm. [Obs.] Spenser. 2. Astonishment; amazement. [Obs.] Spenser. Gay.\n\n1. Hour; time; season. [Obs.] Chaucer. 2. A brief space of time; a moment. [Obs.] Chaucer. In a stound, suddenly. [Obs.] Chaucer.\n\nA vessel for holding small beer. [Prov. Eng.] Halliwell.
