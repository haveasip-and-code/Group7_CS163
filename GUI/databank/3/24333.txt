replenish
1. To fill again after having been diminished or emptied; to stock anew; hence, to fill completely; to cause to abound. Multiply and replenish the earth. Gen. i. 28. The waters thus With fish replenished, and the air with fowl. Milton. 2. To finish; to complete; to perfect. [Obs.] We smothered The most replenished sweet work of nature. Shak.\n\nTo recover former fullness. [Obs.] The humors will not replenish so soon. Bacon.
