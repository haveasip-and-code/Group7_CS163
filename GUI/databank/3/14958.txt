mister
A title of courtesy prefixed to the name of a man or youth. It is usually written in the abbreviated form Mr. To call your name, inquire your where, Or whet you think of Mister Some-one's book, Or Mister Other's marriage or decease. Mrs. Browning.\n\nTo address or mention by the title Mr.; as, he mistered me in a formal way. [Colloq.]\n\n1. A trade, art, or occupation. [Obs.] In youth he learned had a good mester. Chaucer. 2. Manner; kind; sort. [Obs.] Spenser. But telleth me what mester men ye be. Chaucer. 3. Need; necessity. [Obs.] Rom. of R.\n\nTo be needful or of use. [Obs.] As for my name, it mistereth not to tell. Spenser.
