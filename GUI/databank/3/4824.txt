kin
A diminutive suffix; as, manikin; lambkin.\n\nA primitive Chinese instrument of the cittern kind, with from five to twenty-five silken strings. Riemann.\n\n1. Relationship, consanguinity, or affinity; connection by birth or marriage; kindred; near connection or alliance, as of those having common descent. 2. Relatives; persons of the same family or race. The father, mother, and the kinbeside. Dryden. You are of kin, and so a friend to their persons. Bacon.\n\nOf the same nature or kind; kinder. \"Kin to the king.\" Shak.
