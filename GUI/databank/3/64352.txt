lowly
1. Not high; not elevated in place; low. \"Lowly lands.\" Dryden. 2. Low in rank or social importance. One common right the great and lowly claims. Pope. 3. Not lofty or sublime; humble. These rural poems, and their lowly strain. Dryden. 4. Having a low esteem of one's own worth; humble; meek; free from pride. Take my yoke upon you, and learn of me, for I am meek and lowly in heart. Matt. xi. 29.\n\n1. In a low manner; humbly; meekly; modestly. \"Be lowly wise.\" Milton. 2. In a low condition; meanly. I will show myself highly fed, and lowly taught. Shak.
