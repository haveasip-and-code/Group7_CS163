dreary
1. Sorrowful; distressful. [Obs.] \" Dreary shrieks.\" Spenser. 2. Exciting cheerless sensations, feelings, or associations; comfortless; dismal; gloomy. \" Dreary shades.\" Dryden. \"The dreary ground.\" Prior. Full many a dreary anxious hour. Keble. Johnson entered on his vocation in the most dreary part of that dreary interval which separated two ages of prosperity. Macaulay.
