drowse
To sleep imperfectly or unsoundly; to slumber; to be heavy with sleepiness; to doze. \"He drowsed upon his couch.\" South. In the pool drowsed the cattle up to their knees. Lowell.\n\nTo make heavy with sleepiness or imperfect sleep; to make dull or stupid. Milton.\n\nA slight or imperfect sleep; a doze. But smiled on in a drowse of ecstasy. Mrs. Browning.
