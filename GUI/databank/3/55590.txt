surcease
Cessation; stop; end. \"Not desire, but its surcease.\" Longfellow. It is time that there were an end and surcease made of this immodest and deformed manner of writing. Bacon.\n\nTo cause to cease; to end. [Obs.] \"The waves . . . their range surceast.\" Spenser. The nations, overawed, surceased the fight. Dryden.\n\nTo cease. [Obs.]
