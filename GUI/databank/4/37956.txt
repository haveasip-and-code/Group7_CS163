mook (n.)
(noun) - a less powerful enemy, easily dealt with, often appearing in large groups to present some challenge; a thug. Used especially in the context of pen-and-paper roleplaying games. ; Background: ; - Possibly first used in the roleplaying milieu in the game 'Feng Shui' by Robin Laws. ; - Possibly derived from 'mook jung', the wooden training dummy used in wing chun style martial arts. ; Note: ; Other definitions for this word likely exist.
