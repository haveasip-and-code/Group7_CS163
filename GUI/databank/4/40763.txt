five by five
Five by five is a radio communications expression that means 'loud and clear'. One of the fives represents the S units of reception strength. The other five is a rating of the signal clarity. Five by five is a good, clear signal. The radio use of this expression goes back to the 1950s: ''All right, testing, one-two-three-four. . .' 'Five by five, Mr. Holloran!'' (Hunter, Blackboard Jungle, 1954). ; The more metaphorical meaning, popularized by Faith Lehane from the television show Buffy the Vampire Slayer, wasn't recorded until the 1980s. ; The use of the word is primarily used in the military, or when discussing something similar.
