corset
An undergarment, first coming to view in the [Rennaissance], that women have worn to mangle their natural body into the shape of an [hourglass]: corsets pinched the waist in, pushed the [breasts] up, and rearranged the spine and abdominal organs. ; The corset used to be made of whalebone for support, but it now comes in a variety of fabrics. ; Women have had many health problems and have died from the [restrictive], [impractical] corset; many homes in the [Victorian] era had [faint]ing rooms for women who had been laced up too tightly. ; Nowadays, the corset is popular among the [gothic] crowd and has become a symbol of [erotica].
