ghettify
v. (from [ghetto]) ; 1. To allow or promote degeneration of a community, through the efflux of the middle class and legitimate business and the influx of a culture of poverty, crime, low ambition, shiftlessness, and [bling]. Opposite of 'gentrify.' ; 2. To modify a possession such as a car or sound system to reflect the styles of the aforementioned 'ghetto culture.'
