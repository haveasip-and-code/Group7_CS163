hic-a-belch
1-n. A term for a bodily function in which one begins to hiccup (sounds like 'HIC-...'), which fills the lungs with air, then on the release, instead of a painful knotting of the diaphragm muscles as in a normal hiccup, a [belch] is released. This can result in very impressive belching results, if the timing is perfected. ; 2-v. To perform said function.
