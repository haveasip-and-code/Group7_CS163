suilk
(verb) pronounced: swilk. ; to swallow, gulp, or suck with a slobbering noise; to swallow food with an abnormal amount of noise; to eat like a hog at a trough. ; Originally of Scottish origin, suilk has come to be used in English, and have English verb conjugations. Thus, 'suilking' is the act, and a 'suilker' is one who suilks. ; Commonly used as an insult or provocation.
