red handed
adj. Derived from the term 'bloody-hand', which was one of four kinds of offences in the king's forest, by which the offender is supposed to have killed a deer. In Scotland, in such like crimes they say 'Taken in the fact, or with the red hand'. ; - Thomas Blount's Law Dictionary and Glossary, 1717 ; In modern times, this term refers to catching someone in the act of doing something they aren't supposed to do.
