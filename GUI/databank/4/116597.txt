hang together
1. To [hang out] together, as in a [clique], either out of loyalty or attraction to one another, out of disdain for others, or out of desperation when no one else will [hang] with you. ; 2. To [take the heat] together, either out of loyalty to one another, or when given no choice. Derives from the stereotypical [lynching], which involves [hanging] from a noose.
