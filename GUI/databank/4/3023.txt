hiff
Etymology: Believed to be of Australian origin, describing a grunt of exertion, or perhaps a modification of 'heave'. ; Transitive verb: 'to hiff (something)': ; 1. To throw (something) in a cumbersome manner, often something heavy or uncooperative. ; 2. To dispose of something, often something that has exceeded its usefulness. ; Rarely used intransitively, such as in 'Dave hiffs'.
